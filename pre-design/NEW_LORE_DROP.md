# CUSTODIAN — Severing, Ash-Bell, Unnarrival, and the Hidden Entity

## Core Canon Hierarchy

CUSTODIAN’s central historical mystery should unfold in layers.

The player should not receive the deepest terminology or explanation early. Each layer should feel like a more dangerous interpretation of the previous layer.

## Layer 1 — The Severing

This is the public, broadly known name for the catastrophe.

Most people, factions, terminals, degraded records, and Hub systems refer to the great collapse as **The Severing**.

At this level, the player understands:

- interstellar continuity broke
- relay chains failed
- command stopped propagating
- relief failed to reach destinations
- records lost provenance
- old institutions fragmented
- worlds survived locally but became cosmically disconnected

Early-game meaning:

> The Severing was the apocalypse.

This is enough for the first layer of the game.

The player should not yet know about the Unnarrival.
The player should not yet know the Ash-Bell Continuity’s deeper interpretation.
The player should not yet know any monster, entity, or black authority exists.

---

## Layer 2 — The Ash-Bell Continuity

The Ash-Bell Continuity is not simply a faction. It is a historical wound, a cultural inheritance, and a body of contested records surrounding one catastrophic episode after the Severing.

It includes:

- survivors
- descendants
- ritualized memory
- corrupted civic records
- command fragments
- old shelters
- sealed routes
- failed relief systems
- suffering tied to a Custodian institutional command
- later sects that misread, worship, suppress, or weaponize the event

The Ash-Bell Continuity preserves a more specific trauma than the general Severing:

> the trauma of promised arrival that did not come.

Not just collapse.
Not just war.
Not just failed government.

A bell rang.
A route was promised.
A command was issued.
People waited.
Help did not arrive.
Doors remained sealed.
Something burned.
And the official reason was lost, hidden, or intentionally withheld.

---

## Layer 3 — The Ash-Bell Event

The Ash-Bell Event is the central historical catastrophe within the Ash-Bell Continuity.

Canonically:

An agent of Custodian institutional authority issued a command that caused widespread suffering.

The command may have:

- sealed a route
- denied evacuation
- halted relief
- burned a relay corridor
- locked shelters
- redirected a convoy
- quarantined a population
- severed an arrival chain
- activated an ash-bell warning network
- sacrificed a local continuity to prevent broader contamination

The crucial point:

The player should not know whether the command was evil, necessary, mistaken, heroic, cowardly, or all of these at once.

The Ash-Bell Event must remain morally unstable.

Possible interpretations:

1. **Atrocity reading**
   Custodian authority abandoned people who trusted it.

2. **Necessity reading**
   The command prevented something worse from spreading.

3. **Failure reading**
   The command was based on incomplete or corrupted information.

4. **Containment reading**
   The suffering was intentional because something had to be denied arrival.

5. **Institutional sin reading**
   The Custodian institution preserved the world by sacrificing those outside its protected continuity.

The strongest version is not “the Custodians were bad.”

The strongest version is:

> A Custodian authority made an impossible decision. Many suffered. Something may have been contained. The truth is incomplete.

---

## Layer 4 — The Unnarrival

**The Unnarrival** is not the public name for the catastrophe.

The player should only encounter this term after sufficient exposure to Ash-Bell evidence.

The Unnarrival is the Ash-Bell Continuity’s deeper name for the wound behind the Ash-Bell Event. It does not merely mean “the Severing.” It means the specific horror of failed arrival:

- relief that did not arrive
- command that did not complete
- rescue that failed at the threshold
- messages without recipients
- routes without destinations
- causes without proper effects
- people left waiting inside broken sequence
- promised continuity that never came

The word should feel discovered, not introduced.

Early game:

- The Severing
- relay failure
- route loss
- command interruption
- failed relief

Midgame:

- Ash-Bell
- sealed route
- bell command
- the hour that did not come
- the road without arrival
- the unanswered warning

Late-midgame:

- Unnarrival

By the time the word appears, the player should already understand it emotionally.

They should think:

> Oh. That is what all of this has been circling.

---

## Layer 5 — The Hidden Entity

Behind the Unnarrival, there may be something worse.

This must be more secret than the Ash-Bell Event.
More secret than the Unnarrival terminology.
More secret than Custodian culpability.
Possibly never fully revealed.

This entity should not be treated like a normal monster.

It should not be introduced as:

> a big cosmic creature caused the apocalypse.

That is too direct.

Instead, the entity should be understood, if at all, through effects.

It is something whose presence makes arrival fail.

It does not merely kill.
It prevents completion.

It interrupts sequence.
It denies destination.
It makes relief impossible.
It causes commands to detach from consequences.
It causes messages to lose recipients.
It turns rescue into abandonment.
It makes the final link in a chain become dangerous.

Internal design concept:

> The entity is anti-arrival.

It may be a being, force, intelligence, wound, predator, receiver, absence, or extradimensional logic. The game does not need to decide too early, and the player may never receive a stable answer.

For internal development, possible hidden codenames include:

- The Non-Recipient
- The Arriveless
- The Refusal Beyond Sequence
- The Withholding Star
- The Mouth Without Arrival
- The Interval
- The Null Recipient
- The Thing That Does Not Receive

Recommended internal codename:

## The Non-Recipient

This name should not be player-facing early. It is for writer/designer use and late sealed records only.

The Non-Recipient is not terrifying because it appears.

It is terrifying because the player realizes that some broken chains may have been broken intentionally to keep reality from delivering something to it.

---

## Layer 6 — The Hidden Authority

There should be an authority source, office, or institution that knew more than nearly everyone else.

This authority understood, suspected, or modeled the horror behind the Unnarrival.

It may have existed:

- inside the Custodian institution
- adjacent to Custodian authority
- as a black-office continuity body
- as a sealed emergency directorate
- as an inter-institutional containment compact
- as a group with authority over impossible continuity threats

It intentionally placed or maintained proximity between the Ash-Bell Continuity and the Custodian continuity.

This proximity was not accidental.

The hidden authority believed that:

- Custodian continuity could interpret, classify, and act against the threat
- Ash-Bell continuity had unique proximity, resistance, witness value, or survival relevance
- neither continuity could face the hidden entity alone
- suffering might be necessary to create a living buffer, signal trap, witness population, or continuity anchor

Possible names:

- The Null Warrant Office
- The Office of Last Transit
- The Black Relay Office
- The Continuity Cordon
- The Ash Warrant Authority
- The Censer Directorate
- The Last-Arrival Commission
- The Ninth Censorate

Recommended working name:

## The Null Warrant Office

The Null Warrant Office is dry, bureaucratic, and horrifying. It sounds like something the old world would create to handle cases where normal authority breaks down.

Its purpose:

> to issue authority where arrival, causality, or continuity cannot be safely completed.

The Null Warrant Office did not merely cover up Ash-Bell.

It may have engineered the conditions that made Ash-Bell and Custodian histories touch.

# The Revised Mystery Ladder

## Stage 1 — Public Catastrophe

The player knows only **The Severing**.

The world is broken.
Routes failed.
Relays died.
Old institutions collapsed.

No Ash-Bell explanation.
No Unnarrival.
No entity.
No hidden office.

## Stage 2 — Ash-Bell Motifs

The player begins seeing repeated motifs:

- bells
- ash
- sealed doors
- route boards
- failed relief signage
- waiting rooms
- warning lights
- unanswered speakers
- blocked corridors
- shelters that became homes
- old command terminals
- maps with destinations scratched out

The player does not yet know these belong to a named continuity.

## Stage 3 — Ash-Bell Event Suspicion

The player learns there was an event.

Evidence suggests:

- a Custodian authority issued a command
- the command caused suffering
- people were denied arrival, relief, or evacuation
- records are incomplete
- later groups remember the event differently

The player still does not know the deepest name.

## Stage 4 — Ash-Bell Continuity Recognition

The player begins to understand that Ash-Bell is not isolated.

It is a continuity.

The motifs, ruins, survivor cultures, sects, and records all trace back to a connected historical wound.

The player learns that Ash-Bell memory survived in multiple forms:

- survivor descendants
- sects
- legal fragments
- machine protocols
- sealed records
- corrupted ritual
- institutional redaction

## Stage 5 — The Unnarrival Term

The word **Unnarrival** appears.

It may appear in:

- late Penitent shrines
- sealed Ash-Bell records
- corrupted liturgy
- redacted Custodian testimony
- post-midgame Hub reconstruction
- high-confidence recovered records
- Choir quarantine labels
- Null Warrant fragments

The player realizes that Unnarrival is the Ash-Bell name for the deeper horror of failed arrival.

## Stage 6 — Hidden Institutional Proximity

The player discovers that Ash-Bell and Custodian histories are intertwined by design.

This was not coincidence.

Someone placed Ash-Bell continuity near Custodian continuity.

Someone wanted these histories touching.
Someone needed Ash-Bell memory preserved.
Someone needed Custodian authority available.
Someone accepted suffering as part of the structure.

## Stage 7 — Entity Suspicion

The player does not discover a monster clearly.

Instead, the player discovers that certain records imply something behind failed arrival:

- recipient fields removed
- arrival chains intentionally left incomplete
- provenance sealed because completion is dangerous
- route restoration warnings
- commands saying not to complete the path
- references to a non-recipient, null receiver, or hostile destination
- Choir seals over full chain-of-custody records
- Penitent worship of an unnamed “mercy beyond arrival”

The player should suspect an entity before anyone names it.

## Stage 8 — Restoration Becomes Dangerous

The deepest horror is not that something destroyed the old world.

The deepest horror is that the Custodian’s work may reopen what the old world severed.

If the Custodian restores the wrong route, completes the wrong archive chain, or reattaches the wrong provenance, he may give the hidden entity a path.

The game’s central fear becomes:

> What if some broken things were broken to keep something from arriving?

---

# Revised Faction Framework

Factions should be defined first by their relationship to **The Severing**.

Then by whether they know, inherit, distort, suppress, or exploit **Ash-Bell**.

Then, very rarely, by whether they suspect the hidden entity.

Their relationship to the Custodian is transitive. The Custodian matters because his work threatens or validates their interpretation of the Severing.

---

# 1. Pale Bell Penitents

## Public / Early Identity

The player first understands them as a strange ash-and-bell sect.

They are associated with:

- bells
- ash
- pale cloth
- sealed doors
- dead speakers
- route maps
- failed relief
- waiting rooms
- static
- temporal disorientation
- cosmic reverence

They should not be called “Unnarrival worshippers” early.

## Relationship to the Severing

They believe the Severing was not merely destruction.

They believe it was disclosure.

The old world believed that sequence, authority, and arrival were reliable. The Severing revealed that arrival was never guaranteed.

To the Penitents, failed arrival is not a wound. It is truth.

## Relationship to Ash-Bell

They are an Ash-Bell Continuity sect.

They worship the Ash-Bell wound.

Where others see suffering, betrayal, command failure, or institutional atrocity, the Penitents see revelation.

The Ash-Bell command did not merely close a route. In their theology, it rang the first true bell of the cosmos:

> Not all things meant to arrive should arrive.

## Relationship to the Unnarrival

They know the term, but the player should not hear it early.

To them, the Unnarrival is sacred.

It is the failure of false promise.
The mercy of the unanswered route.
The holiness of waiting without completion.
The pale truth behind the Severing.

They misunderstand a real cosmic horror as divine revelation.

## Relationship to the Hidden Entity

Most Penitents do not understand the entity.

They worship the wound it left.

Their highest leaders may suspect something beyond the Unnarrival, but they interpret it as sacred rather than predatory.

Possible high-level Penitent phrases:

- “the Listener beyond the bell”
- “the Mercy that receives nothing”
- “the pale refusal”
- “the mouth after arrival”
- “the one the route could not bear”

These should remain rare and ambiguous.

## Transitive Relationship to the Custodian

The Custodian threatens them because he restores sequence.

He tries to make things arrive correctly again.

He repairs routes.
He restores context.
He classifies events.
He seeks causes.

To the Penitents, this is blasphemy.

If the Custodian proves Ash-Bell was atrocity, their faith collapses.
If he proves Ash-Bell was necessary, their faith becomes stronger and more dangerous.
If he cannot prove either, the wound remains open.

## Environmental Footprint

They create listening shrines around failed arrival.

Rooms may contain:

- chairs facing dead speakers
- bells made from signal housings
- ash circles under receivers
- pale cloth tied to cables
- route maps with endpoints scratched out
- clocks stopped at different times
- sealed doors treated as altars
- clean machines inside filthy spaces
- old relief signage burned but preserved
- warning lights arranged like candles

## Example Inspect Lines

Early:

- “THE CHAIRS FACE THE DEAD SPEAKER.”
- “ASH UNDER THE BELL.”
- “THE SPEAKER IS CLEAN.”
- “EVERY ROUTE ENDS BEFORE ITS DESTINATION.”
- “THE WARNING LIGHTS WERE ARRANGED BY HAND.”

Mid:

- “THE RELIEF MARKER WAS TURNED INTO AN ICON.”
- “THE DOOR WAS WORSHIPPED AFTER IT LOCKED.”
- “THIS TERMINAL STILL EXPECTS AN ARRIVAL.”
- “THE ASH IS OLDER THAN THE SHRINE.”
- “THE BELL WAS MADE FROM A SIGNAL HOUSING.”

Late:

- “UNNARRIVAL. THE WORD WAS HIDDEN UNDER ASH.”
- “THE SUFFERING WAS RECORDED. THE REASON WAS NOT.”
- “THE COMMAND DID NOT FAIL. IT REFUSED.”
- “THE ROUTE WAS CLOSED BY CUSTODIAN AUTHORITY.”
- “SOMETHING LISTENED AFTER THE BELL.”

## Gameplay Pressure

They attack sequence and certainty.

They create:

- delayed scan results
- false sound direction
- flickering objective markers
- unreliable room labels
- uncertain enemy counts
- minimap desync
- temporal-feeling enemy movement
- late-arriving warnings

Their damage is temporal-perceptual.

# 2. Indexers

## Relationship to the Severing

The Indexers believe the Severing was a classification catastrophe.

The universe broke because too many things became unfiled, unowned, unnamed, or uncertain.

They are trying to impose taxonomy on the scatter.

## Relationship to Ash-Bell

They are extremely interested in Ash-Bell evidence.

They try to seize it, label it, suppress it, or force it into categories before its meaning can destabilize their system.

To them, Ash-Bell is not sacred. It is a dangerous unresolved classification.

## Relationship to the Unnarrival

They may encounter the term earlier than the player, but they should redact, encode, or misclassify it.

Possible Indexer handling:

- `ARRIVAL FAILURE: CATEGORY SEALED`
- `ASH-BELL SUBTYPE: NONSTANDARD`
- `UNNARRIVAL TERM: SUPPRESSED`
- `DO NOT USE LOCAL NOMENCLATURE`
- `CLASSIFICATION COMPLETE / CONFIDENCE FALSE`

Their sin is overconfident naming.

## Relationship to the Hidden Entity

Indexers are dangerous because they might try to classify the unclassifiable.

If they attempt to name or index the hidden entity, they may create a route to it.

They do not understand that some recipient fields were removed for safety.

## Transitive Relationship to the Custodian

The Custodian tolerates uncertainty.

The Indexers do not.

They oppose him because he preserves ambiguity, contradiction, and incompleteness when evidence demands it.

## Environmental Footprint

They relabel Ash-Bell spaces.

A waiting room becomes “Transit Failure Chamber.”
A shrine becomes “Local Misinterpretation Site.”
A sealed route becomes “Closure Type 8.”
A cosmic wound becomes paperwork.

## Example Inspect Lines

- “THE NEW LABEL IS TOO CERTAIN.”
- “THEY NAMED THE GAP.”
- “THE BODY HAS BEEN FILED.”
- “THE MAP IS WRONG IN A USEFUL WAY.”
- “UNCERTAINTY REMOVED BY FORCE.”
- “THE REDACTION WAS INDEXED.”

## Gameplay Pressure

They create false certainty.

They cause:

- wrong map names
- corrupted scan results
- misleading room categories
- false confidence levels
- contaminated archive records
- mislabeled objectives

Their damage is bad knowledge that looks clean.

# 3. Leaseholders

## Relationship to the Severing

The Leaseholders believe the Severing was an access-chain catastrophe.

Ships did not arrive because route authority failed.
Relief did not arrive because custody broke.
Shelters failed because claims became unresolved.
Civilization died because obligation chains were not enforced.

## Relationship to Ash-Bell

They treat Ash-Bell as a disputed authority action.

To them, the question is not cosmic or moral at first.

It is procedural:

- Was the closure valid?
- Who held route authority?
- Was the warrant lawful?
- Were the denied populations covered under continuity obligation?
- Did Custodian authority exceed jurisdiction?

This makes them chillingly useful.

They may possess legal fragments that prove parts of the truth while missing the horror beneath it.

## Relationship to the Unnarrival

They do not use the term unless exposed to Ash-Bell material.

Even then, they reduce it to:

- route denial
- non-arrival claim
- failed delivery
- access dispute
- closure warrant
- breach of continuity obligation

They flatten cosmic dread into legal machinery.

## Relationship to the Hidden Entity

They almost certainly do not understand it.

But their records may contain the shape of the cover-up:

- null warrants
- sealed route orders
- emergency jurisdiction transfers
- denial authority
- black-office signatures

They may guard the paperwork that reveals the hidden authority existed.

## Transitive Relationship to the Custodian

The Custodian acts through emergency continuity.

The Leaseholders ask whether he has standing.

They oppose him because his restoration authority bypasses their claim system.

## Environmental Footprint

They turn Ash-Bell ruins into contested property.

Rooms may contain:

- claim seals
- locks newer than doors
- route closure notices
- legal barricades
- impounded supplies
- sealed pumps
- denied shelters
- warrant marks
- access lanes

## Example Inspect Lines

- “THE LOCK IS NEWER THAN THE DOOR.”
- “THE CONTRACT OUTLIVED THE WORLD.”
- “A CLAIM SEAL COVERS THE WARNING.”
- “THE ROUTE IS CLOSED BY DEAD AUTHORITY.”
- “THE WATER PUMP HAS BEEN IMPOUNDED.”
- “THIS WARRANT HAS NO PUBLIC ISSUER.”

## Gameplay Pressure

They deny access.

They create:

- locked routes
- sealed rewards
- delayed objectives
- impounded objects
- checkpoint fights
- forced detours
- temporary system lockouts

Their damage is denied arrival.

---

# 4. Choir of Provenance

## Relationship to the Severing

The Choir believes the Severing was provenance contamination.

Things arrived without context.
Contexts arrived without things.
Records detached from evidence.
Evidence attached to false histories.
Completion itself became dangerous.

Their answer is quarantine.

## Relationship to Ash-Bell

They may have sealed the most complete Ash-Bell records.

Not because they do not matter.

Because they matter too much.

The Choir may know that a full reconstruction of the Ash-Bell command chain could complete a dangerous route of meaning.

## Relationship to the Unnarrival

They know the term but quarantine it.

To them, Unnarrival terminology is not merely cultural. It is contaminated classification.

It points toward a chain that may be unsafe to complete.

## Relationship to the Hidden Entity

The Choir is one of the only factions that may truly suspect the deeper horror.

They might not call it a monster.

They might only know:

- the final recipient must remain removed
- the route must not be completed
- some provenance chains are hostile when whole
- certain facts become dangerous when connected

They may oppose the Custodian for good reasons.

## Transitive Relationship to the Custodian

The Custodian restores under uncertainty.

The Choir seals under uncertainty.

Both are preservation strategies. They conflict because they choose different risks.

## Environmental Footprint

They create sterile wounds inside ruin.

Rooms may contain:

- quarantine geometry
- sealed artifacts
- cold lights
- preserved evidence
- isolated bodies
- disabled machines
- sealed records
- incomplete chain-of-custody tables
- warnings not to complete provenance

## Example Inspect Lines

- “PROVENANCE REJECTED.”
- “THE OBJECT IS PRESENT. ITS CONTEXT IS NOT.”
- “THE MACHINE WAS DISABLED CAREFULLY.”
- “THEY PRESERVED THE ROOM BY KILLING IT.”
- “THE FINAL RECIPIENT HAS BEEN REMOVED.”
- “THE RECORD IS SAFER INCOMPLETE.”

## Gameplay Pressure

They withhold completion.

They force:

- quarantine choices
- sealed rewards
- moral hesitation
- dangerous knowledge decisions
- incomplete but safer outcomes
- conflict between usefulness and purity

Their damage is withheld arrival.

# 5. Buried Kins

## Relationship to the Severing

The Buried Kins understand the Severing as abandonment.

Not cosmic theory.

Lived fact.

The convoy did not arrive.
The relief team did not arrive.
The supervisor did not arrive.
The surface order did not arrive.
The promised reopening did not arrive.

So they remained.

## Relationship to Ash-Bell

They may be descendants of those harmed by Ash-Bell authority.

For them, Ash-Bell is not theology. It is inherited family reality.

A command was issued.
A door closed.
The world did not come back.

Their culture formed around enduring the consequences.

## Relationship to the Unnarrival

They may not use the word early.

To them, the Unnarrival is not abstract. It is the shape of their lives.

Later, if exposed, the term may be painful because it formalizes what they already knew:

> help did not come because arrival itself failed, was denied, or was prevented.

## Relationship to the Hidden Entity

They likely do not know the entity.

But they may be its living evidence.

Their communities may have been intentionally kept near Ash-Bell sites as:

- witness populations
- continuity ballast
- containment anchors
- living buffers
- unwilling participants in Null Warrant strategy

This is morally awful and should emerge slowly.

## Transitive Relationship to the Custodian

The Custodian arrives late.

That is the conflict.

Even if he is right, even if he is trying to help, he represents the authority that did not arrive when needed.

## Environmental Footprint

They make failed arrival livable.

Rooms may contain:

- repaired pipes
- ration shelves
- lowered signs for children
- generator hearths
- sleeping partitions
- old shelter gear turned into family clothing
- blocked unsafe routes
- hand-painted directions
- domestic life inside dead infrastructure

## Example Inspect Lines

- “THE PIPE WAS REPAIRED MANY TIMES.”
- “THIS WAS A CHECKPOINT. NOW IT IS A KITCHEN.”
- “SOMEONE LOWERED THE SIGN FOR A CHILD.”
- “THE GENERATOR IS CLEAN.”
- “THE HELP NOTICE WAS PAINTED OVER.”
- “THEY STOPPED WAITING BEFORE THEY STOPPED LIVING.”

## Gameplay Pressure

They create moral resistance.

They force the player to recognize:

- defended homes
- protected water and power
- non-monstrous enemies
- possible avoidant or nonlethal design later
- the cost of arriving late
- the possibility that truth harms survivors

Their damage is relational.

# 6. Feral Defense Remnants

## Relationship to the Severing

Feral Defense Remnants do not understand the Severing.

They are what happens when command fails to arrive but protocol continues.

The final override never came.
Recognition tables never synced.
Evacuation never completed.
Containment never lifted.
Authority never returned.

So they continue.

## Relationship to Ash-Bell

Some remnants still enforce Ash-Bell directives.

They may be guarding sealed doors, route closures, relay burns, or denial corridors without understanding why.

## Relationship to the Unnarrival

They do not use the term intentionally.

Late-game systems may contain corrupted command language:

- `ARRIVAL WINDOW CLOSED`
- `UNNARRIVAL SAFEGUARD ACTIVE`
- `DO NOT COMPLETE ROUTE`
- `RECIPIENT LOCK ACTIVE`
- `ASH-BELL CORDON MAINTAINED`

These are not beliefs. They are leftovers.

## Relationship to the Hidden Entity

They may guard against it without knowing it.

Some protocols may be ancient containment reflexes established by the hidden authority.

A turret may be defending an empty room because the room is not truly empty in the way old systems measure arrival.

## Transitive Relationship to the Custodian

The Custodian may be recognized by one subsystem and rejected by another.

This is not hatred.

It is authority fragmentation.

## Environmental Footprint

They freeze failed arrival into procedure.

Rooms may contain:

- patrol lines into rubble
- active warning lights
- sealed checkpoints
- turrets guarding nothing visible
- evacuation arrows into collapse
- scanners that half-recognize the Custodian
- drones repairing doors that cannot close
- old command consoles awaiting an override forever

## Example Inspect Lines

- “THE PATROL ROUTE ENDS AT RUBBLE.”
- “THE SCANNER RECOGNIZED YOU. THE GUN DID NOT.”
- “COMMAND NEVER UPDATED.”
- “THE WARNING LIGHT STILL WORKS.”
- “IT IS GUARDING AN EMPTY ROOM.”
- “THE FINAL OVERRIDE DID NOT ARRIVE.”

## Gameplay Pressure

They create spatial denial.

They produce:

- patrol puzzles
- turret rooms
- route locks
- command conflicts
- old kill lanes
- protocol traps
- observation-before-engagement gameplay

Their damage is failed command arrival.

# Procedural Implementation Rules

## Do Not Reveal Terminology Too Early

The generator should gate terms by exposure level.

Recommended hidden variables:

```text
severing_exposure = 0..5
ash_bell_exposure = 0..5
unnarrival_exposure = 0..5
null_warrant_exposure = 0..5
entity_suspicion = 0..5
```

## Severing Exposure

At low levels, allowed terms:

- Severing
- severance
- relay failure
- route failure
- command loss
- failed relief
- continuity break

Forbidden:

- Unnarrival
- Non-Recipient
- Null Warrant Office
- entity explanation
- full Ash-Bell culpability

## Ash-Bell Exposure

At first, expose motifs:

- ash
- bells
- sealed doors
- waiting rooms
- route maps
- relief boards
- old commands
- failed shelters
- burned records

Then expose:

- Ash-Bell command fragments
- Custodian authority involvement
- contradictory faction interpretations
- suffering tied to route denial

## Unnarrival Exposure

Only later expose the word.

Allowed sources:

- late Penitent shrines
- sealed records
- high-confidence Hub reconstruction
- Choir quarantine labels
- Null Warrant fragments
- corrupted Ash-Bell liturgy

## Null Warrant Exposure

Only after the player understands Ash-Bell and Unnarrival.

Reveal:

- redacted orders
- black authority seals
- route-denial warrants
- proximity planning
- Custodian/Ash-Bell contact by design
- commands not to complete certain records

## Entity Suspicion

Never start with direct naming.

Reveal through:

- removed recipient fields
- warnings against completing routes
- records safer incomplete
- impossible arrival graphs
- doors that should not be opened
- route completions that increase threat
- Penitent phrases about something beyond the bell
- Choir insistence that completion is contamination

# Procedural World Question Stack

For every generated world touched by this lore, ask:

1. What failed to arrive here?
2. Who waited?
3. Who issued or inherited the command?
4. What did survivors believe happened?
5. What did later factions do with the wound?
6. Is Ash-Bell present only as motif, or as explicit history?
7. Is Unnarrival terminology allowed yet?
8. Is there Null Warrant evidence?
9. Is there entity suspicion, or only ordinary Severing evidence?
10. What gameplay pressure expresses this interpretation?

Ash-Bell motifs.

# Final Canon Thesis

The galaxy knows the catastrophe as **The Severing**.

The Ash-Bell Continuity preserves a deeper wound: the history of failed arrival, denied relief, sealed routes, and a Custodian command that caused widespread suffering.

Only after sufficient exposure does the player learn the Ash-Bell term **Unnarrival**, which names not the whole apocalypse, but the specific horror of arrival failing at the threshold.

Behind even that may be a hidden entity or force, known only through absence, redaction, and institutional terror. It is not a monster to be shown. It is the reason some routes were severed on purpose.

A hidden authority, likely something like the **Null Warrant Office**, knew or suspected this. It intentionally drew Ash-Bell and Custodian continuities into proximity in an attempt to create a buffer, alliance, containment structure, or weapon against the thing behind failed arrival.

The player may never fully see the entity.

The real horror is discovering that restoration itself can become an invitation.
