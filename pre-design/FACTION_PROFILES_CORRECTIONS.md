# CUSTODIAN — Factions by Relationship to the Severing / Unarrival

## Core Framing

The major factions of CUSTODIAN should not be defined first by their relationship to the Custodian.

They should be defined by their relationship to the Severing / Unarrival.

The Severing was not merely an apocalypse. It was a cosmic-scale break in arrival, continuity, sequence, and rational inheritance. Things that should have arrived did not. Things that had already happened lost their proper place. Signals reached destinations without causes. Institutions retained procedures without authority. Worlds inherited fragments without context. Temporal and civic rationality were scattered across the universe like debris.

Every major faction is an answer to that wound.

The Custodian threatens them not because he is personally important, but because he represents a forbidden possibility:

that the broken sequence may be interpreted, partially restored, or judged.

# 1. The Unarrived Penitents

Formerly: The Penitents of Static

## Core Concept

The Unarrived Penitents are a cosmic devotional order built around a partial, terrifying insight into the Unarrival event.

They understand, more than most factions, that the collapse was not merely political, military, or technological. They know something happened to sequence itself. They know that arrivals failed. They know that messages, people, histories, and meanings were scattered from their proper destinations. They know that the universe did not simply fall apart — it failed to complete.

But they misunderstand this insight as revelation.

To them, the Unarrival is not a wound to be healed. It is the true face of the cosmos.

They worship the moment things failed to arrive.

## Relationship to the Severing / Unarrival

The Penitents believe the Severing was a divine disclosure.

Before the Unarrival, they believe civilization lived inside a false comfort: cause followed effect, messages reached recipients, institutions recognized authority, names pointed to things, and time pretended to be continuous.

The Unarrival shattered that lie.

In their theology, the scattered condition of the universe is not damage. It is truth finally revealed. The universe was never whole. Sequence was always a cage. Arrival was always an arrogance.

They call the Severing by sacred names:

- The Great Non-Arrival
- The Pale Interruption
- The Mercy of Unsequence
- The Shattering of Direction
- The Hour That Did Not Come

## What They Are Right About

They have perceived a real cosmic-scale event.

They understand that the collapse involved more than infrastructure failure. They recognize that causality, transmission, memory, and institutional continuity were all damaged at once. They are sensitive to temporal residue, failed arrivals, repeated signals, and places where sequence is thin.

They often find Unarrival sites before anyone else.

They are not fools. Their rituals are built around real phenomena.

## What They Are Wrong About

They mistake wound for revelation.

They believe that because the Unarrival exposed the fragility of ordered meaning, ordered meaning itself must be false. They worship the break instead of trying to understand it.

Where the Custodian sees scattered context, they see sacred distribution.

Where the Hub sees lost provenance, they see liberation from provenance.

Where survivors see tragedy, they see initiation.

## Transitive Relationship to the Custodian

The Custodian is horrifying to them because he is an agent of arrival.

He restores routes.
He reattaches names.
He repairs chains of trust.
He classifies events.
He turns sacred scatter back into sequence.

To the Penitents, this is not merely wrong. It is blasphemy against the revelation of the Unarrival.

They do not hate the Custodian because he preserves the old world. They hate him because he threatens to make the universe arrive again.

## Visual Identity

The Unarrived Penitents should feel more cosmic and uncanny than simple electrical cultists.

They still use static, signal noise, damaged speakers, antennas, and broken transmissions, but these are devotional instruments — ways of listening to failed arrival.

They should evoke:

- blindfolded or veiled listeners
- pilgrimage through broken signal zones
- ritual antenna frames
- pale signal cloth
- ash and white dust
- tuning devices
- temporal scar markings
- bodies wrapped as if preparing for a journey that never completes
- half-maintained machines that hum like shrines

They should feel like a collision of industrial ruin and cosmic insight.

Not generic cultists.
Not dirty raiders.
Not fantasy mages.

They are people who heard the universe fail to complete and decided to kneel.

## Material Condition

Ritual-worn, not merely filthy.

Some are ash-stained and ragged. Others are carefully wrapped, pale, composed, and almost monastic. Their sacred machines may be immaculately maintained while their bodies are neglected.

Their visual condition should say:

“We do not preserve ourselves. We preserve the interruption.”

## Color Language

- pale ash
- dirty white
- charcoal
- dead signal gray
- bruised violet
- muted cosmic blue
- sickly amber
- weak red indicator light
- cold white glow

Use less orange-rust junkyard energy than before. Give them a more uncanny, pale, devotional presence.

## Unit Archetypes

### Listener

Basic melee unit. Veiled, wire-wrapped, carries a shock blade or pilgrimage staff. Moves like someone guided by sound more than sight.

### Signal Pilgrim

Basic ranged unit. Uses a coil rifle, static projector, or tuned signal weapon. Their firearm should feel like a listening device turned violent.

### Failed Arrival Heavy

Armored bruiser wrapped in heavy signal cloth and old insulation. Carries a large humming receiver, bell-frame, or weighted shock instrument.

### Unsequence Runner

Fast skirmisher. Light, twitchy, wrapped in pale streamers or signal cords. Should feel as if moving half a beat out of normal rhythm.

### Tuning Acolyte

Support unit. Carries antenna arrays, speaker relics, or tuning forks. Disrupts map clarity, scan confidence, and target certainty.

### Pale Witness

Elite unit. More composed, almost serene. A veteran who has “seen” an Unarrival scar and survived. Uses precise, ritualized violence.

### Interruption Hierophant

Leader. Tall, veiled, shrine-like silhouette with antenna halo, broken receiver mantle, or pale signal frame.

### Non-Arrival Vessel

Special unit. A living jammer / temporal distortion bearer. Its presence makes room labels, enemy counts, and objective markers unreliable.

## Environmental Footprint

The Unarrived Penitents do not merely occupy rooms. They convert them into listening chambers for failed causality.

They leave:

- speaker shrines
- antenna circles
- pale cloth strips tied to cables
- ash rings around signal equipment
- walls covered in repeated arrival marks crossed out
- clocks stopped at impossible or inconsistent times
- chairs arranged facing dead receivers
- broken monitors preserved as holy objects
- maps with routes scratched out before their endpoints
- doors marked as “not yet”
- machines intentionally made to hum, buzz, or repeat

Their rooms should feel like places where people are waiting for something that will never arrive.

## Procedural Room Modifiers

When the Unarrived Penitents modify a room, add:

- pale signal cloth
- ash circles
- antenna clusters
- dead speaker arrays
- repeated clock motifs
- crossed-out route signs
- tuned humming machines
- broken receivers
- low flickering white/blue light
- static distortion particles
- ritual listening seats
- “arrival denied” style symbols, but without readable text if possible

## Enemy Behavior

Before combat, they may:

- kneel toward a dead transmitter
- stand under static light
- tune a speaker
- circle a clock or receiver
- wrap cable around a doorway
- listen to a wall
- drag a machine into alignment
- strike a metal surface at timed intervals
- ignore the player briefly if a signal event is active

In combat, they should:

- interfere with information
- desynchronize player expectations
- jam objective markers
- create false sound cues
- cause brief map uncertainty
- attack relays or arrival-linked machines
- defend Unarrival scars or signal shrines

## Lore Exposure Style

Never have them explain the Unarrival directly.

Let the player see:

- a room full of chairs facing a dead speaker
- a route map where every destination is scratched away
- enemies kneeling beneath static
- a machine kept clean only because it produces noise
- clocks stopped at different impossible times
- a corpse wrapped like a traveler, placed before a sealed door
- a terminal repeating an arrival notice for someone who never came

The player should slowly realize:

these people are not worshipping static.

They are worshipping the failure of arrival.

The Unarrived Penitents attack sequence and certainty.

# 2. The Indexers

## Relationship to the Severing / Unarrival

The Indexers believe the Severing happened because the universe was insufficiently classified.

To them, Unarrival was not divine revelation or cosmic horror. It was administrative failure at impossible scale.

Messages failed to arrive because chains were not enforced.
People failed to arrive because identities were not pinned.
Histories scattered because categories were loose.
Truth decayed because records were allowed to remain uncertain.

The Indexers are not trying to understand the wound. They are trying to label the wound until it behaves.

## What They Are Right About

The Severing did create catastrophic ambiguity. Provenance failure did matter. If no one can classify what survived, restoration becomes dangerous.

The Indexers understand that unstructured fragments become lethal.

## What They Are Wrong About

They believe a false classification is better than an open question.

They would rather impose a complete map of lies than admit the universe contains unresolved damage.

## Transitive Relationship to the Custodian

The Custodian is dangerous because he preserves uncertainty as a valid state.

He does not label everything immediately.
He distinguishes evidence, inference, contradiction, and loss.
He refuses to canonize what cannot yet be known.

The Indexers see this as cowardice. To them, the Custodian is a failed classifier with obsolete authority.

## Visual Identity

The Indexers should look controlled, semi-maintained, procedural, and invasive.

They should feel like archivists who became occupiers.

## Environmental Footprint

They relabel Unarrival scars.

A room where something failed to arrive becomes “Transit Failure Type 3.”
A shrine becomes “Misallocated Signal Chapel.”
A corpse becomes “Unclaimed Biological Record.”
A cosmic wound becomes paperwork.

## Example Inspect Lines

- “THE NEW LABEL IS TOO CERTAIN.”
- “THEY NAMED THE GAP.”
- “THE MAP IS WRONG IN A USEFUL WAY.”
- “THE BODY HAS BEEN FILED.”
- “UNCERTAINTY REMOVED BY FORCE.”

## Gameplay Identity

Indexers attack ambiguity by replacing it with bad certainty.

They create:

- false labels
- misleading scans
- overconfident bad data
- corrupted map categories
- contaminated archive records
- wrong but clean-looking information

Their damage is false certainty.

# 3. The Leaseholders

## Relationship to the Severing / Unarrival

The Leaseholders believe the Severing was an access-chain catastrophe.

To them, civilization failed because rightful claims were broken. Ships did not arrive because routes lost authority. Cargo did not arrive because custody chains fractured. People did not arrive because jurisdiction collapsed.

They interpret the Unarrival as the greatest breach of contract in history.

Their answer is enforcement.

## What They Are Right About

Infrastructure does require authority. Routes, depots, gates, relays, and shelters need access control. Without custody, systems become predatory or useless.

The Leaseholders understand that civilization depends on obligation.

## What They Are Wrong About

They confuse dead contracts with living responsibility.

They would enforce an ancient claim over a present need. They would lock a water purifier because the ownership chain is unresolved.

## Transitive Relationship to the Custodian

The Custodian acts under emergency continuity doctrine.

The Leaseholders ask: by whose lease, whose title, whose access grant, whose route authority?

They oppose the Custodian because he represents discretionary restoration outside their claim system.

## Environmental Footprint

They turn Unarrival sites into disputed property.

A failed transit bay becomes a claims office.
A lost convoy becomes impounded cargo.
A sealed shelter becomes contested tenancy.
A cosmic rupture becomes an unresolved jurisdictional event.

## Example Inspect Lines

- “THE LOCK IS NEWER THAN THE DOOR.”
- “A CLAIM SEAL COVERS THE WARNING.”
- “THE ROUTE IS CLOSED BY DEAD AUTHORITY.”
- “THE WATER PUMP HAS BEEN IMPOUNDED.”
- “THE CONTRACT OUTLIVED THE WORLD.”

## Gameplay Identity

Leaseholders attack access.

They create:

- locked routes
- sealed rewards
- delayed objectives
- confiscated objects
- access denial fields
- legal barricades
- checkpoint combat

Their damage is denied arrival.

# 4. The Choir of Provenance

## Relationship to the Severing / Unarrival

The Choir of Provenance believes the Severing was contamination.

To them, the Unarrival scattered broken truths across the universe. Some things arrived without context. Some contexts arrived without things. Some histories became detached from their evidence. Some evidence became attached to false histories.

Their answer is quarantine.

They do not worship the Unarrival. They fear its residue.

## What They Are Right About

They understand the danger of restoring corrupted knowledge.

They know that not every recovered thing should be trusted. They know that a broken truth can infect future interpretation.

## What They Are Wrong About

They would rather preserve purity than life.

They mistake total quarantine for wisdom. They may seal away the only thing that could help because it cannot be proven clean.

## Transitive Relationship to the Custodian

The Custodian operates under uncertainty.

The Choir sees this as contamination risk. To them, the Custodian is dangerously willing to touch scattered fragments and rejoin them into working meaning.

The Custodian restores.
The Choir seals.

## Environmental Footprint

They sterilize Unarrival sites.

They preserve evidence, seal artifacts, mark contamination, isolate bodies, disable useful machines, and maintain cold boundaries around suspect truths.

## Example Inspect Lines

- “SEALED AFTER ARRIVAL.”
- “THE OBJECT IS PRESENT. ITS CONTEXT IS NOT.”
- “PROVENANCE REJECTED.”
- “THE MACHINE WAS DISABLED CAREFULLY.”
- “THEY PRESERVED THE ROOM BY KILLING IT.”

## Gameplay Identity

The Choir attacks choice.

They force the player to decide whether to:

- break quarantine
- recover contaminated knowledge
- trust partial provenance
- sacrifice usefulness for safety
- preserve sealed truth or restore it

Their damage is withheld arrival.

# 5. The Buried Kins

## Relationship to the Severing / Unarrival

The Buried Kins believe the Severing was abandonment.

To them, Unarrival is not an abstract cosmic wound. It is the day help did not come.

The convoy did not arrive.
The relief signal did not arrive.
The supervisors did not arrive.
The surface did not arrive.
The promised reopening did not arrive.

So they remained.

Their culture formed around the moral authority of endurance.

## What They Are Right About

They survived when larger systems failed them.

They maintained lights, water, ration cycles, shelter doors, tool chains, and family continuity. Their truth is local, embodied, and practical.

## What They Are Wrong About

They may mistake survival for total truth.

Because no one came back, they built meaning around remaining sealed. Any outside interpretation threatens the dignity of what they endured.

## Transitive Relationship to the Custodian

The Custodian arrives too late.

That is the first and deepest conflict.

He may be authorized.
He may be correct.
He may even be helpful.

But he represents the world that failed to arrive when they needed it.

## Environmental Footprint

They domesticate Unarrival.

A failed shelter becomes a home.
A maintenance wing becomes a kitchen.
A checkpoint becomes a nursery wall.
A sealed corridor becomes a family boundary.
A ration protocol becomes tradition.

## Example Inspect Lines

- “THE PIPE WAS REPAIRED MANY TIMES.”
- “THIS WAS A CHECKPOINT. NOW IT IS A KITCHEN.”
- “SOMEONE LOWERED THE SIGN FOR A CHILD.”
- “THE GENERATOR IS CLEAN.”
- “THE HELP NOTICE WAS PAINTED OVER.”

## Gameplay Identity

The Buried Kins attack intrusion.

They create:

- defended living spaces
- barricaded homes
- protected infrastructure
- retreat routes
- guarded water/power sources
- moral hesitation
- possible nonlethal or avoidant solutions later

Their damage is late arrival.

# 6. Feral Defense Remnants

## Relationship to the Severing / Unarrival

Feral Defense Remnants do not understand the Severing.

They are what remains when authority fails to arrive but protocol continues.

Orders did not update.
Command did not arrive.
Evacuation did not complete.
Recognition tables did not sync.
The final override never came.

So they continue.

They are the machinery of arrival stuck in a dead loop.

## What They Are Right About

Some of what they guard is still dangerous.

Their protocols may be obsolete, but not always baseless. The old world sealed many things for a reason.

## What They Are Wrong About

They cannot reinterpret.

They enforce context after context has died.

## Transitive Relationship to the Custodian

The Custodian may be recognized by one subsystem and rejected by another.

This makes them uncanny. They are not enemies because they hate him. They are enemies because the world that would have confirmed him failed to arrive.

## Environmental Footprint

They freeze Unarrival into procedure.

A patrol route continues into rubble.
A turret guards an empty desk.
A scanner accepts the Custodian while the gun beside it fires.
Evacuation arrows point into collapsed halls.
A maintenance drone repairs a door that can never close.

## Example Inspect Lines

- “THE PATROL ROUTE ENDS AT RUBBLE.”
- “THE SCANNER RECOGNIZED YOU. THE GUN DID NOT.”
- “COMMAND NEVER UPDATED.”
- “THE WARNING LIGHT STILL WORKS.”
- “IT IS GUARDING AN EMPTY ROOM.”

## Gameplay Identity

Feral Defense Remnants attack movement.

They create:

- patrol zones
- turret logic
- locked checkpoints
- old kill lanes
- protocol traps
- spatial denial
- observation-before-engagement gameplay

Their damage is failed command arrival.

# Implementation Principle

Each faction should be generated procedurally by its response to Unarrival, not by static backstory.

For every generated world, ask:

1. What failed to arrive here?
2. Who interpreted that failure?
3. What did they build around the absence?
4. What physical footprint did that belief leave?
5. How does that belief affect combat, navigation, information, or player choice?

That keeps lore indirect, procedural, and playable.

The **Unarrived Penitents** are much stronger than the old Static version. “Static” is now just one ritual technology they use; the real faction identity is that they worship the cosmic failure of arrival itself.
