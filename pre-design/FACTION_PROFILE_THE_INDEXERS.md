THE INDEXERS.

Use all shared CUSTODIAN style instructions above.

FACTION IDENTITY:
The Indexers are organized, invasive, procedural, and possessive. They are archive raiders, relabelers, evidence thieves, and classification zealots. They should feel like people who conquer through categorization, seizure, and imposed order. They are not wild scavengers. They are controlled, deliberate, and quietly oppressive.

VISUAL DIRECTION:

- semi-maintained field uniforms mixed with industrial archive gear
- layered coats, aprons, utility harnesses, segmented armor plates
- tags, seals, coded strips, label tabs, numbered plates, scanner modules, record cases, docket satchels
- masks or visors with narrow analytical lenses
- tools adapted into weapons: marking rifles, seizure hooks, breach cutters, catalog staves, industrial batons
- precise silhouettes, tidy loadouts, deliberate posture
- asymmetry is okay, but should feel functional rather than chaotic

MATERIAL CONDITION:

- worn but organized
- used, dented, and field-tested
- not filthy
- not luxurious
- clearly maintained because their work depends on control and legibility

COLOR LANGUAGE:

- dull steel, soot gray, faded white, muted tan, dirty yellow, institutional black
- restrained accents such as red tags, orange paint marks, or pale cyan scanner lights

MOOD:
cold, invasive, methodical, forensic, bureaucratic, territorial

MAKE THE 8 UNITS FEEL LIKE:

1. seizure trooper with hooked baton or impound tool
2. ranged tagging operative with marking rifle
3. breach heavy with slab armor and industrial cutter
4. swift filing-runner / scout with compact weapon and data tabs
5. archive technician carrying scanner rig or retrieval device
6. veteran relabeler with reinforced visor and precise gear
7. adjudicator / overseer with commanding coat silhouette
8. special unit: catalog brute, data-harvester, or mobile indexing frame

IMPORTANT:

- they should look like they overwrite ownership and identity wherever they go
- strong roster cohesion
- clean gameplay silhouettes
