# THIS DOCUMENT COVERS CORRECTIONS TO THE PRIOR LORE IMPLEMENTATIONS, ALL LORE MUST FIT WITH THE FOLLOWING RULING BEFORE HARDENING AS A DESIGN DOCUMENT.

The mistake in the previous pass was letting **“Unnarrival” terminology surface too early**. That word should feel like a deeper, later-keyed revelation from the **Ash-Bell Continuity**, not the baseline name every faction uses.

The player-facing early term should be:

## The Severing

That is the widely known historical/cosmic catastrophe.

Later, after repeated exposure to Ash-Bell material, the player realizes:

## The Unnarrival

…is not just a poetic synonym. It is the **Ash-Bell Continuity’s interpretation** of what the Severing actually was: not only a break, but a failure of arrival, relief, command, sequence, and promised continuity.

That lets the mystery unfold in layers.

Also, since your project guidance says active design authority lives in the Godot docs/design stack and meaningful runtime/canon shifts should update the active AI context pack, this exact terminology split should eventually become a lore/design doc entry, not just chat lore.

# Revised canon model

## Layer 1: Common historical term

**The Severing**

This is what most factions, terminals, old system logs, and generalized Hub classifications call it.

Meaning:

- interstellar continuity broke
- trust chains failed
- command structures fractured
- provenance collapsed
- many things stopped reaching their intended destinations
- old civilization became locally survivable but cosmically incoherent

Early-game player understanding:

> The Severing was the apocalypse.

Good. Simple. Usable.

## Layer 2: Institutional Custodian term

The Custodian institution probably uses dry, procedural language.

Examples:

- **Severance Event**
- **Continuity Failure**
- **Lattice Severance**
- **Authenticated Context Collapse**
- **Relay-Civic Failure**
- **Interregional Arrival Loss**

This should appear in degraded terminals, Hub language, command records, and official logs.

Early example:

> SEVERANCE EVENT CONFIRMED.
> RELAY CHAIN UNTRUSTED.
> LOCAL AUTHORITY UNRESOLVED.

No poetry yet.

## Layer 3: Ash-Bell Continuity term

**The Unnarrival**

This term should be hidden until the player has enough contact with Ash-Bell-derived evidence.

It should not appear in the first hour unless extremely obscured, corrupted, or not yet meaningful.

The player first sees symptoms:

- failed relief routes
- bells without callers
- sealed shelters
- command records
- ash-marked civic spaces
- old emergency announcements
- survivors referring to “the hour that did not come”
- repeated imagery of waiting, command, and abandonment

Only later does the player encounter the word:

> UNNARRIVAL

And by then it lands.

Because the player already understands the pattern before receiving the name.

# The Ash-Bell Continuity

This is now one of the strongest pieces of CUSTODIAN lore.

The Ash-Bell Continuity is not just a faction. It is a historical-cultural wound.

It consists of:

- survivors
- later inheritors
- ritualized records
- hostile sects
- machine procedures
- ruins
- legal fragments
- songs/signals/alarms
- corrupted civic memory
- arguments over whether an ancient command was atrocity, necessity, or both

The Ash-Bell Continuity centers around three linked ideas:

1. **The Unnarrival**
   The failure of promised arrival: relief, command, evacuation, authority, rescue, restoration, or sequence.

2. **The Ash-Bell Event**
   A specific historical catastrophe in which a Custodian institutional authority issued a command that caused widespread suffering.

3. **The unresolved verdict**
   It remains unclear whether that command prevented something worse.

That is the key.

Do **not** make it “Custodians bad.”
Make it:

> A Custodian authority made a decision under impossible conditions. Many suffered. Something may have been contained. The truth is incomplete.

That is much better.

# The Ash-Bell Event

## Core idea

During or after the Severing, a Custodian institutional agent issued an emergency command.

This command may have:

- sealed a route
- halted a relief convoy
- rerouted evacuation
- burned a contaminated relay chain
- denied entry to survivors
- locked shelters
- activated an ash-bell warning network
- quarantined a whole continuity branch
- sacrificed a population to prevent wider propagation

The command caused enormous suffering.

But the player should not know whether it was:

- a catastrophic error
- a cruel but necessary quarantine
- an act of institutional cowardice
- a heroic sacrifice remembered as betrayal
- a containment protocol whose prevented disaster is now unknowable

That ambiguity is very CUSTODIAN.

# Why “Ash-Bell” works

The bell is not just religious flavor.

The bell should mean:

- warning
- arrival signal
- quarantine signal
- civic summons
- emergency authority
- mourning
- sequence marker
- a signal that should be answered

Ash means:

- aftermath
- burned records
- scorched continuity
- body-scale suffering
- sterilization
- purification
- ruin
- evidence destroyed by necessity or crime

Together:

## Ash-Bell = the warning that came, but help did not.

That is extremely strong.

# Revised faction framework

Each faction should now be defined by:

1. Their relationship to **The Severing**
2. Whether they know, inherit, distort, or suppress **Ash-Bell Continuity**
3. How they interpret the possibility that the Custodian institution once caused suffering
4. Why the Custodian becomes a transitive threat

So we do not lead with “they hate the Custodian.”

We lead with:

> What did the Severing mean to them?

Then:

> What does the Custodian’s return imply about that meaning?

# The Pale Bell Penitents

This gives the Bloodborne-adjacent feeling without exposing “Unnarrival” yet.

## Early-game player understanding

The Pale Bell Penitents are an eerie signal/cosmic cult associated with bells, ash, failed routes, pale cloth, static, and temporal disorientation.

They seem to worship the Severing.

They speak around absence, waiting, and failed summons.

They do not explain themselves.

## Late-game revealed understanding

They are an Ash-Bell Continuity sect.

They believe the Severing’s truest name is **the Unnarrival**.

They worship the cosmic failure of arrival as revelation.

They believe the Ash-Bell Event was not an atrocity, but the first clear ringing of the universe’s real law:

> Not all things meant to arrive should arrive.

That is terrifying.

# Pale Bell Penitents profile

## Relationship to the Severing

They believe the Severing was not merely collapse.

They believe it was disclosure.

Before the Severing, civilization lived under a false assumption: that signal, command, relief, memory, history, and authority could travel correctly.

The Severing revealed that arrival was never guaranteed.

They worship that revelation.

## Relationship to the Ash-Bell Event

They believe the Ash-Bell command was sacred.

Where others see betrayal, they see initiation.

A Custodian authority issued a command. People suffered. Doors closed. Routes burned. Relief failed. Shelters went unanswered.

The Penitents say:

> Yes. That was when the bell rang true.

They do not necessarily know the full truth. They have inherited ritualized fragments of the event.

They may possess partial records that make them more dangerous because they are not entirely wrong.

## Hidden relationship to the Unnarrival

The word **Unnarrival** should only appear in:

- late Penitent shrines
- Ash-Bell archive fragments
- corrupted liturgy
- sealed Custodian testimony
- post-midgame Hub reconstruction
- high-confidence recovered records

Early Penitent language should use indirect phrases:

- “the hour that did not come”
- “the road without arrival”
- “the bell after the route”
- “the pale waiting”
- “the mercy of the unanswered”
- “the silence beyond command”
- “the kindness of closed gates”

Then later the player realizes all these point to **Unnarrival**.

## Transitive relationship to the Custodian

The Custodian is not hated because he is simply “old authority.”

He is hated because his return threatens to reopen the verdict.

If the Custodian can prove the Ash-Bell command was wrong, the Penitents’ faith collapses.

If he can prove it was necessary, their worship becomes more dangerous.

If he cannot prove either, then the player remains trapped in the same unresolved wound.

That is much more interesting.

# Revised cross-faction Severing model

## The Pale Bell Penitents

**Severing interpretation:** cosmic disclosure
**Ash-Bell relationship:** worshippers of the Ash-Bell wound
**Unnarrival relationship:** hidden sacred name revealed later
**Gameplay pressure:** temporal-perceptual distortion, failed arrival, delayed certainty

## The Indexers

**Severing interpretation:** classification catastrophe
**Ash-Bell relationship:** they try to file and relabel Ash-Bell evidence before its meaning is understood
**Unnarrival relationship:** they may know the word as a forbidden classification term before the player does, but should redact or encode it
**Gameplay pressure:** false certainty, mislabeled rooms, corrupted records

Example:

- early: `ARRIVAL FAILURE: CATEGORY SEALED`
- late: `UNNARRIVAL: INDEX SUPPRESSED`

## The Leaseholders

**Severing interpretation:** access-chain breach
**Ash-Bell relationship:** they treat the Ash-Bell command as a disputed authority action
**Unnarrival relationship:** irrelevant or legally inconvenient; they reduce it to warrants, closures, and route denial
**Gameplay pressure:** denied access, sealed routes, impounded resources

Example:

- “THE ROUTE WAS CLOSED LEGALLY.”
- “THE DEAD WERE NOT ABANDONED. THEY WERE DENIED ENTRY.”

## The Choir of Provenance

**Severing interpretation:** provenance contamination
**Ash-Bell relationship:** they may have sealed the most complete Ash-Bell records
**Unnarrival relationship:** known but quarantined
**Gameplay pressure:** withheld truth, sealed evidence, moral choice

Example:

- `ASH-BELL RECORD: PROVENANCE CONTESTED`
- `UNNARRIVAL TERMINOLOGY QUARANTINED`

## The Buried Kins

**Severing interpretation:** abandonment
**Ash-Bell relationship:** they are the descendants of people who waited for help that never arrived
**Unnarrival relationship:** not a cosmic doctrine to them at first; it is lived history
**Gameplay pressure:** defended homes, moral hesitation, late-arrival guilt

Example:

- “THE HELP NOTICE WAS PAINTED OVER.”
- “THEY STOPPED COUNTING DAYS AFTER THE BELL.”

## Feral Defense Remnants

**Severing interpretation:** none; protocol continued without command
**Ash-Bell relationship:** may still enforce Ash-Bell command fragments
**Unnarrival relationship:** appears as corrupted command language in late-game systems
**Gameplay pressure:** spatial denial, old protocols, command ghosts

Example:

- `ASH-BELL DIRECTIVE ACTIVE`
- `ARRIVAL WINDOW CLOSED`
- `COMMAND SOURCE LOST`
- late: `UNNARRIVAL SAFEGUARD: DO NOT OPEN`

# How to reveal the terminology procedurally

This is the important implementation part.

Do not have a lore dump say:

> The Ash-Bell people called it the Unnarrival.

Instead, use a terminology unlock ladder.

## Stage 1: Severing

Common logs and Hub language use:

- Severing
- Severance Event
- Continuity failure
- Relay loss
- Arrival failure
- Route closure

## Stage 2: Ash-Bell motifs

The player repeatedly sees:

- ash
- bells
- sealed routes
- failed relief
- waiting rooms
- unanswered signals
- command fragments
- suffering tied to old authority

## Stage 3: Ash-Bell Event

The player discovers:

- a Custodian authority issued a command
- the command caused suffering
- the command affected routes, shelters, or relief
- the justification is missing or sealed

## Stage 4: Contradictory interpretations

Different factions frame it differently:

- Penitents: sacred refusal
- Indexers: classification failure
- Leaseholders: valid/invalid closure warrant
- Choir: quarantined provenance
- Buried Kins: abandonment
- Defense Remnants: active protocol

## Stage 5: Unnarrival term

Only after enough evidence:

- the Hub can reconstruct the Ash-Bell term
- late shrines show it
- sealed records mention it
- Penitent leaders use it
- old Custodian command fragments contain it as a warning, not theology

# Clean canon correction

Use this as the corrected canon:

The galaxy knows the catastrophe as **The Severing**.

The **Ash-Bell Continuity** preserves a more specific and more dangerous interpretation: **the Unnarrival**, the failure of promised arrival, command, relief, and sequence.

The **Ash-Bell Event** was a historical crisis in which an agent of Custodian institutional authority issued a command that caused widespread suffering. Whether that command was atrocity, necessity, or both remains unresolved.

The Pale Bell Penitents worship the Ash-Bell wound. They are not merely static cultists; they are believers in the sacredness of failed arrival. But the player should not understand that fully until enough Ash-Bell exposure has accumulated.

That is the version I would lock.
