THE BURIED KINS visual design template & faction roster source

Use the shared CUSTODIAN industrial sci-fi pixel art style:

- top-down / slight 2.5D gameplay angle
- readable for a real action / tactics game
- handcrafted pixel art, not painterly
- strong silhouettes, practical gear, grounded materials
- consistent proportions, lighting, pixel density, and perspective
- transparent background
- no scenery, no UI, no text
- lineup / spritesheet presentation only

FACTION IDENTITY:
The Buried Kins are descendants of sealed shelters, continuity bunkers, underground service populations, and long-isolated survival communities. They are not horror creatures, not boogeymen, and not generic mutants. They should feel human, practical, defensive, and shaped by generations of enclosed living, recycled infrastructure, and inherited survival culture.

They are a people who endured.

VISUAL DIRECTION:

- practical shelter clothing layered with inherited bunker gear
- old utility jackets, reinforced workwear, respirators, lamps, patched enclosure suits, gloves, belts, storage pouches
- equipment that looks repaired over generations, carefully reused, and kept functional
- maintenance tools, tunnel tools, old service hardware, shelter-access devices repurposed into weapons
- compact silhouettes suited to enclosed spaces, maintenance tunnels, service corridors, and survival labor
- visual hints of lineage, role, and community responsibility rather than monster design
- subtle environmental adaptation is okay, but keep them clearly human and grounded

MATERIAL CONDITION:

- worn, reused, patched, inherited, carefully maintained
- scarcity wear, not monstrous grime
- survival-made, functional, and practical
- gear should look old but valued

COLOR LANGUAGE:

- bunker green, faded orange, work gray, earth brown, rust, dirty cream, muted navy
- restrained accents like weak utility blue, dim red indicator lights, faded safety yellow, or old civic striping

MOOD:
defensive, resilient, wary, practical, communal, enclosed, post-collapse working-class continuity

THEY SHOULD FEEL LIKE:

- descendants of workers, technicians, wardens, recyclers, shelter guards, medics, and maintenance crews
- people whose whole culture formed around keeping sealed systems running
- suspicious outsiders, but not monstrous
- tragic because they preserved life through isolation and hardship

MAKE THE 8 UNITS FEEL LIKE:

1. basic tunnel defender with improvised close weapon or service tool
2. ranged enclave guard with handmade firearm or compact launcher
3. armored shelter heavy in inherited protective gear
4. fast runner / courier / scout built for tight corridors
5. recycler / technician / field repair specialist
6. veteran defender with carefully preserved elite equipment
7. warden / elder / continuity leader with symbolic survival gear
8. special unit: exo-rig worker, sealed-door breacher, or emergency-response relic bearer

IMPORTANT:

- keep them human-first, not monster-first
- avoid horror tropes, feral poses, or creepy creature energy
- do not lean into fungus, mutation, or cave-ghoul aesthetics
- make them look like a believable isolated society shaped by bunker life, maintenance labor, and generational survival
- the tragedy should come from their history and material condition, not from making them visually monstrous
