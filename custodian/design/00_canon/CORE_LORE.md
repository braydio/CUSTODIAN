# CUSTODIAN — Core Lore Canon

> **Status:** Canonical design reference — single source of truth for all lore
> **Supersedes:** All pre-design lore documents. Contradictions resolved per ASH_BELL_AND_DESIGN_GUIDANCE.md (final authority), FACTION_PROFILES_CORRECTIONS.md (secondary authority).
> **Terminology lock:** This document uses the corrected terminology ladder. "Penitents of Static" replaced by "Pale Bell Penitents" (early) / "Unarrived Penitents" (late). "The Severance" corrected to "The Severing" (public term).

---

## Core Thesis

CUSTODIAN's central historical mystery unfolds in layers. The player should not receive the deepest terminology or explanation early. Each layer should feel like a more dangerous interpretation of the previous layer.

The galaxy knows the catastrophe as **The Severing**.

The **Ash-Bell Continuity** preserves a deeper wound: the history of failed arrival, denied relief, sealed routes, and a Custodian command that caused widespread suffering.

Only after sufficient exposure does the player learn the Ash-Bell term **Unnarrival**, which names not the whole apocalypse, but the specific horror of arrival failing at the threshold.

Behind even that may be a hidden entity or force, known only through absence, redaction, and institutional terror. A hidden authority — the **Null Warrant Office** — knew or suspected this and intentionally drew Ash-Bell and Custodian continuities into proximity.

The real horror is discovering that restoration itself can become an invitation.

---

## Layer 1 — The Severing (Public Catastrophe)

This is the broadly known name for the collapse. Most people, factions, terminals, degraded records, and Hub systems refer to the great collapse as **The Severing**.

**Player understanding at this layer:**
- Interstellar continuity broke
- Relay chains failed
- Command stopped propagating
- Relief failed to reach destinations
- Records lost provenance
- Old institutions fragmented
- Worlds survived locally but became cosmically disconnected

**Early-game meaning:** The Severing was the apocalypse.

**Forbidden at this layer:** Unnarrival terminology, Ash-Bell Continuity, hidden entity, Null Warrant Office, full Custodian culpability.

---

## Layer 2 — The Ash-Bell Continuity (Historical Wound)

The Ash-Bell Continuity is not simply a faction. It is a historical wound, a cultural inheritance, and a body of contested records surrounding one catastrophic episode after the Severing.

**It includes:**
- Survivors and descendants
- Ritualized memory and corrupted civic records
- Command fragments and old shelters
- Sealed routes and failed relief systems
- Suffering tied to a Custodian institutional command
- Later sects that misread, worship, suppress, or weaponize the event

**Core trauma:** The trauma of promised arrival that did not come. Not just collapse — a bell rang, a route was promised, a command was issued, people waited, and help did not arrive.

---

## Layer 3 — The Ash-Bell Event (Historical Catastrophe)

An agent of Custodian institutional authority issued a command that caused widespread suffering. The command may have:

- Sealed a route
- Denied evacuation
- Halted relief
- Burned a relay corridor
- Locked shelters
- Redirected a convoy
- Quarantined a population
- Severed an arrival chain
- Sacrificed a local continuity to prevent broader contamination

**Crucial constraint:** The player should NOT know whether the command was evil, necessary, mistaken, heroic, cowardly, or all of these at once. The Ash-Bell Event must remain **morally unstable**.

**Possible interpretations (all valid, none confirmed):**
1. **Atrocity reading** — Authority abandoned people who trusted it
2. **Necessity reading** — The command prevented something worse from spreading
3. **Failure reading** — The command was based on incomplete information
4. **Containment reading** — Something had to be denied arrival
5. **Institutional sin reading** — The Custodian institution preserved the world by sacrificing those outside its protected continuity

**Design principle:** The strongest version is not "the Custodians were bad." The strongest version is: "A Custodian authority made an impossible decision. Many suffered. Something may have been contained. The truth is incomplete."

---

## Layer 4 — The Unnarrival (Ash-Bell Deep Term)

**Not the public name.** The player should only encounter this term after sufficient exposure to Ash-Bell evidence.

The Unnarrival is the Ash-Bell Continuity's deeper name for the wound behind the Ash-Bell Event. It does not merely mean "the Severing." It means the specific horror of failed arrival:

- Relief that did not arrive
- Command that did not complete
- Rescue that failed at the threshold
- Messages without recipients
- Routes without destinations
- Causes without proper effects
- People left waiting inside broken sequence
- Promised continuity that never came

**The word should feel discovered, not introduced.** By the time it appears, the player should already understand it emotionally.

---

## Layer 5 — The Non-Recipient (Hidden Entity)

Behind the Unnarrival, there may be something worse. This is more secret than the Ash-Bell Event, more secret than Unnarrival terminology, more secret than Custodian culpability — possibly never fully revealed.

**This entity should not be treated like a normal monster.** It should be understood, if at all, through effects:

- It is something whose presence makes arrival fail
- It does not merely kill — it prevents completion
- It interrupts sequence, denies destination, makes relief impossible
- It causes commands to detach from consequences
- It causes messages to lose recipients
- It turns rescue into abandonment

**Internal design concept:** The entity is anti-arrival.

**Recommended internal codename: The Non-Recipient.** This name should not be player-facing early. It is for writer/designer use and late sealed records only.

The Non-Recipient is not terrifying because it appears. It is terrifying because the player realizes that some broken chains may have been broken intentionally to keep reality from delivering something to it.

---

## Layer 6 — The Null Warrant Office (Hidden Authority)

An authority source, office, or institution that knew more than nearly everyone else. It understood, suspected, or modeled the horror behind the Unnarrival.

**Possible nature:**
- Inside the Custodian institution
- Adjacent to Custodian authority
- A black-office continuity body
- A sealed emergency directorate
- An inter-institutional containment compact

**Purpose:** To issue authority where arrival, causality, or continuity cannot be safely completed.

The Null Warrant Office did not merely cover up Ash-Bell. It may have engineered the conditions that made Ash-Bell and Custodian histories touch. It believed:
- Custodian continuity could interpret, classify, and act against the threat
- Ash-Bell continuity had unique proximity, resistance, witness value
- Neither continuity could face the hidden entity alone
- Suffering might be necessary to create a living buffer, signal trap, or continuity anchor

## The Mystery Ladder (8-Stage Player Progression)

### Stage 1 — Public Catastrophe
The player knows only **The Severing**. The world is broken. Routes failed. Relays died. No Ash-Bell explanation, no Unnarrival, no entity, no hidden office.

### Stage 2 — Ash-Bell Motifs
The player begins seeing repeated motifs: bells, ash, sealed doors, route boards, failed relief signage, waiting rooms, warning lights, unanswered speakers, blocked corridors, shelters that became homes, maps with destinations scratched out. The player does not yet know these belong to a named continuity.

### Stage 3 — Ash-Bell Event Suspicion
The player learns there was an event. A Custodian authority issued a command. The command caused suffering. People were denied arrival, relief, or evacuation. Records are incomplete. Different groups remember the event differently.

### Stage 4 — Ash-Bell Continuity Recognition
The player understands that Ash-Bell is not isolated — it is a continuity. The motifs, ruins, survivor cultures, sects, and records all trace back to a connected historical wound.

### Stage 5 — The Unnarrival Term
The word **Unnarrival** appears in late Penitent shrines, sealed records, corrupted liturgy, redacted Custodian testimony, or high-confidence Hub reconstruction. The player realizes this is the Ash-Bell name for the deeper horror.

### Stage 6 — Hidden Institutional Proximity
The player discovers Ash-Bell and Custodian histories are intertwined by design. Someone placed them in proximity intentionally. Someone accepted suffering as part of the structure.

### Stage 7 — Entity Suspicion
The player discovers records implying something behind failed arrival: removed recipient fields, provenance sealed because completion is dangerous, commands saying not to complete the path, references to a non-recipient or null receiver.

### Stage 8 — Restoration Becomes Dangerous
The deepest horror: the Custodian's work may reopen what the old world severed. If he restores the wrong route, completes the wrong archive chain, or reattaches the wrong provenance, he may give the hidden entity a path. **Some broken things were broken to keep something from arriving.**

## Terminology Exposure Ladder (Implementation Guide)

The generator should gate terms by exposure level using hidden variables:

```text
severing_exposure = 0..5
ash_bell_exposure = 0..5
unnarrival_exposure = 0..5
null_warrant_exposure = 0..5
entity_suspicion = 0..5
```

### Severing Exposure Levels

**Level 0 (start):** Allowed: Severing, severance, relay failure, route failure, command loss, failed relief, continuity break. Forbidden: Unnarrival, Non-Recipient, Null Warrant Office, entity explanation, full Ash-Bell culpability.

**Level 1-2:** Allowed: ash motifs, bells, waiting imagery, failed relief imagery. Forbidden: explicit Unnarrival.

**Level 3-4:** Allowed: Ash-Bell references, old command fragments, suffering tied to closure, Custodian authority involvement, contradictory faction readings. Forbidden: direct "the Custodians caused it" certainty.

**Level 5:** Allowed: Unnarrival as Ash-Bell terminology, Penitent theology around non-arrival, Choir quarantine references, direct Ash-Bell Event records, player-facing reconstruction, unresolved net-good/net-harm framing.

### Null Warrant & Entity Exposure

Only after the player understands Ash-Bell and Unnarrival:
- Reveal redacted orders, black authority seals, route-denial warrants, proximity planning
- Entity revealed through removed recipient fields, warnings against completing routes, records safer incomplete, impossible arrival graphs

## Faction Framework (Corrected)

All factions are defined first by their relationship to **The Severing/Unnarrival**, then by whether they know, inherit, distort, suppress, or exploit **Ash-Bell Continuity**, then rarely by whether they suspect **the hidden entity**. Their relationship to the Custodian is transitive — the Custodian matters because his work threatens or validates their interpretation of the Severing.

### The Pale Bell Penitents (Early) / Unarrived Penitents (Late Revelation)
- **Severing interpretation:** Cosmic disclosure — the Severing revealed arrival was never guaranteed
- **Ash-Bell relationship:** Worshippers of the Ash-Bell wound
- **Unnarrival relationship:** Hidden sacred name revealed late
- **Gameplay pressure:** Temporal-perceptual distortion, failed arrival, delayed certainty
- **Custodian transitive:** He restores sequence — to them, this is blasphemy

### The Indexers
- **Severing interpretation:** Classification catastrophe — the universe broke because too many things became unfiled
- **Ash-Bell relationship:** Try to file and relabel Ash-Bell evidence before its meaning is understood
- **Unnarrival relationship:** Know the word as a forbidden classification term but redact/encode it
- **Gameplay pressure:** False certainty, mislabeled rooms, corrupted records
- **Custodian transitive:** He preserves uncertainty — they see this as cowardice

### The Leaseholders
- **Severing interpretation:** Access-chain breach — civilization failed because rightful claims were broken
- **Ash-Bell relationship:** Treat the Ash-Bell command as a disputed authority action
- **Unnarrival relationship:** Irrelevant or legally inconvenient; reduce it to warrants and route denial
- **Gameplay pressure:** Denied access, sealed routes, impounded resources
- **Custodian transitive:** He acts under emergency continuity — they demand paperwork and jurisdiction

### The Choir of Provenance
- **Severing interpretation:** Provenance contamination — things arrived without context
- **Ash-Bell relationship:** May have sealed the most complete Ash-Bell records
- **Unnarrival relationship:** Known but quarantined; they know the term but suppress it
- **Gameplay pressure:** Withheld truth, sealed evidence, moral choice
- **Custodian transitive:** He restores under uncertainty — they seal under uncertainty; both are preservation strategies that conflict

### The Buried Kins
- **Severing interpretation:** Abandonment — not cosmic theory but lived fact
- **Ash-Bell relationship:** Descendants of those harmed by Ash-Bell authority
- **Unnarrival relationship:** Not cosmic doctrine — lived history of waiting for help that never came
- **Gameplay pressure:** Defended homes, moral hesitation, late-arrival guilt
- **Custodian transitive:** He arrives late — that is the conflict

### Feral Defense Remnants
- **Severing interpretation:** None — protocol continued without command
- **Ash-Bell relationship:** May still enforce Ash-Bell command fragments
- **Unnarrival relationship:** Appears as corrupted command language in late-game systems
- **Gameplay pressure:** Spatial denial, old protocols, command ghosts
- **Custodian transitive:** Recognized by one subsystem, rejected by another — authority fragmentation

## Procedural World Question Stack

For every generated world touched by this lore, ask:

1. What failed to arrive here?
2. Who waited?
3. Who issued or inherited the command?
4. What did survivors believe happened?
5. What did later factions do with the wound?
6. Is Ash-Bell present only as motif, or as explicit history?
7. Is Unnarrival terminology allowed yet?
8. Is there Null Warrant evidence?
9. Is there entity suspicion, or only ordinary Severing evidence?
10. What gameplay pressure expresses this interpretation?
