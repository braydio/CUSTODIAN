# HUB WORLD DOCTRINE

**Status:** draft
**Last Updated:** 2026-04-22

---

## Core Thesis

> **The Hub is a ruined eschatological capital where recovered knowledge does not repair the world so much as make its remaining truth visible again.**

---

## 1. What the Hub *Is* in Lore

The hub is the last surviving **city of meaning**.

Not the political capital of an empire. Something more important. It was the place where civilization tried to make itself **legible to eternity**:

- statues of exemplars
- processional roads
- sealed libraries
- towers aligned to impossible skies
- plazas built for vows, memory, adjudication, and record
- heroic architecture meant to declare that truth could outlast catastrophe

Now that project has failed — but not completely.

The Custodian does not live in a town. He walks through the **corpse of a civilizational promise**.

This is not:
- A normal city
- A populated settlement  
- A "safe zone"

This is a **legendary dead capital at the end of history**, still standing only because memory, duty, and pattern have not fully failed yet.

---

## 2. The Design Constraint: Unpopulated

The hub should feel fundamentally unpopulated. Loneliness is a design constraint, not just mood.

Allowed:
- Residual systems (maintenance, monitoring)
- Little wildlife (sparse, not cozy)
- The Custodian

Not allowed:
- Crowds
- Quest-givers
- Ordinary civic life
- Merchants, inns, chatter
- Hub companions

Wildlife exists as:
- Proof that life still threads through the dead order
- Scale contrast against monumental ruins
- Environmental omen in tiny doses

Good candidates:
- Pale birds that later reveal iridescent throats
- Foxlike or deerlike distant shapes
- Tiny scavengers nesting in statues
- Moths or drifting luminous organisms near prismatic zones

---

## 3. What Color Means

> **The world is becoming perceptible again.**

Not "the world is healing." Not moral purification. Not generic restoration.

Color reasserts **distinctions**:
- Heraldry becomes visible
- Sainted stone takes on mineral warmth
- Oxidized bronze separates from dead marble
- Old murals surface from soot
- Sky phenomena begin to refract
- Prismatic light reveals relationships the player could not previously perceive

At first visit, the hub is nearly achromatic because the Custodian — and maybe reality itself — can no longer properly differentiate meaning from ruin.

As knowledge returns, color does not "unlock" like a videogame reward. It **reasserts distinctions**.

By late game, oversaturation should not feel cheerful. It should feel almost unbearable: too much signal, too much revealed structure, too much truth pressing through the world at once.

---

## 4. Color Domains

Color is restored by **domains of meaning**, not evenly:

| Domain | Hue | Triggers |
|--------|-----|----------|
| **Sacrifice** | Red / ember / wine | War, oath, blood-memory, destructive discovery |
| **Authority** | Gold / amber | Canon, law, sanctioned history, validated hypothesis |
| **Cognition** | Blue / violet | Astronomy, archive, distant truth, cold knowledge |
| **Persistence** | Green | Root-pressure, unauthorized life, reclamation, surviving hypothesis |
| **Synthesis** | White / iridescent | Total revelation, dangerous totality, final campaign |

Campaign outcomes add to specific domains:
- One campaign restores bruised violets in the observatory quarter
- Another wakes gold leaf fragments in the civic basilica  
- Another reveals moss-green life across the broken causeways
- Final phases create impossible chromatic interactions between domains

---

## 5. Spatial Layout

Five mythic zones:

### The Road of Witnesses

A grand avenue lined with toppled or eroded statues implying categories of civilization:
- Law, stewardship, memory, war, astronomy, mourning, craft, pilgrimage

Early: silhouettes only
Later: details and pigments emerge

### The Archive Heights

A raised district of sealed libraries, broken observatories, choir-like vaults, record towers.
Conceptually where scenario selection / knowledge interpretation belongs.

### The Sunken Civic Quarter

Collapsed plazas, drowned courtyards, root-broken paving, half-flooded cloisters.
Nature here feels patient, not lushly cozy.

### The Prism Margin

An outer edge where the city falls away into the impossible cosmic landscape.
Skybox and end-of-all-things feeling hits hardest here.

### The Custodian's Approach

A modest but severe route the player traverses repeatedly.
Should become emotionally familiar so changes in hue and atmosphere land hard.

---

## 6. Celestial Logic

- The hub exists under an **abnormal terminal sky**
- Two solar descents that do not behave like normal astronomy
- Sometimes they overlap
- Sometimes one is delayed, producing a doubled dusk
- Atmospheric particulates cause the sky to prism and "firework" **only when enough chromatic information exists to refract**

Early game: effects mostly invisible or muted
Later: recovered chroma gives the atmosphere something to work with

The city does not merely gain color. It gains a **relationship to light**.

---

## 7. Each Return Is a Question

Each return to hub should answer:

> **What kind of truth did this campaign make visible?**

Not:
- "What loot did I get"
- "What vendor unlocked"
- "What faction moved in"

Instead:
- What district changed in legibility
- What color family returned
- What statue can now be identified
- What mural now resolves into meaning
- What celestial event is now visible
- What part of the city now admits a new interpretation

---

## 8. The Oversaturation Ending

Late hub should not be "pretty." It should feel like the city is becoming **too vivid for a dying universe**.

### Option A: Tragic Sublime
The world is still ending, but the Custodian has made it visible again before it disappears.

### Option B: Dangerous Revelation
The recovered truths are so fundamental that reality itself starts to refract around them. The city becomes almost too radiant to inhabit comfortably.

Either way: remains ruined. Even at peak chroma, it is the end of all things.

---

## 9. Tone Guardrails

- **Not** a normal fantasy city with merchants, inns, chatter
- **Not** moral purification — recovered distinction, not virtue
- **Not** too clean — remains ruined even when restored
- **Not** generic "nature wins" — patient reclamation, not colonization
- **Not** silly-neon oversaturation — majestic, uncanny, slightly oppressive

## 11. Fidelity Progression System

### Three Dimensions of Fidelity

Every return to hub raises one or more of:

| Dimension | Effect |
|-----------|--------|
| **Legibility** | The city can be read more accurately — details, paths, functions become distinguishable |
| **Access** | More of the city and its systems become usable — routes, archives, mechanisms open |
| **Instability** | Reality handles the recovered signal less gracefully — pressure builds, ruptures occur |

The first two are restorative. The third is what keeps late game from becoming just "prettier version of the same hub."

### Core Rule

> **As the hub gains chromatic fidelity, it does not simply become more beautiful; it becomes more truthful, more usable, and less stable.**

---

## 11. Gameplay Effects by Fidelity

### 11.1 Architectural Legibility

As fidelity rises, the city becomes easier to parse in useful ways.

**Mechanics:**
- Broken roads, terraces, stairs, aqueduct paths, rooftop connectors become visually distinguishable
- Statue groups and heraldic markers indicate district function
- Hidden doors, sealed archives, lift points, collapsed shortcuts become readable
- Murals or mosaics reveal category information tied to future campaign types

**Gameplay effect:** At low fidelity, player knows "this is ruined capital." At higher fidelity, they can tell "this was the judicial quarter," "this avenue led to observatories," "these towers were signal beacons" — and that influences which campaign hypotheses the hub surfaces next.

---

### 11.2 Better Scenario Intelligence

Color changes how the hub presents campaign opportunities.

| Fidelity | Campaign Intelligence |
|----------|-------------------|
| Low | Vague proposals, broad unknowns |
| Mid | Campaign categories clearer before selection |
| Later | Certain risk types, failure modes, knowledge stakes partially visible |
| Very Late | City cross-references signals, reveals hidden relationships between prior campaigns |

**Example progression:**
- Early: "Bio anomaly detected"
- Later: "Biotech precursor signal shares architectural pattern with Archive District statuary"

This is progression without power creep.

---

### 11.3 District Reactivation

Different color families wake different city functionality:

| Domain | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| **Gold/amber** | Civic/adjudicative spaces — judgment halls, record chambers, doctrinal classification |
| **Blue/violet** | Observatories, archives, cosmological instruments — better campaign forecasting |
| **Green** | Living overgrowth systems — traversal openings, organic bridges, reclaimed wells |
| **Red/wine** | Martial/sacrificial districts — training grounds, reliquaries |

These are not "biomes." They are parts of the capital becoming themselves again.

---

### 11.4 Memory Bleed / Historical Overlays

At certain fidelity thresholds, brief overlays of the city's former state appear:

- Intact banners over ruinous avenues
- Phantom crowds moving along stairs
- Vanished bridges appearing long enough to cross
- Old defensive alignments visible as light traces
- Prior catastrophic events replaying in fragments

**Gameplay use:** Shows hidden paths, reveals lost interactables, foreshadows lore, indicates which architectural elements are structurally "true."

**Constraint:** Not NPC ghosts. Hub remains unpopulated and lonely.

---

### 11.5 Wildlife as Diagnostic Signal

Wildlife is not just ambience — it is diegetic telemetry:

| Signal | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| Pale birds gather | Truth-bearing structures reactivating |
| Foxlike creatures avoid | Sectors about to destabilize |
| Iridescent moths | Usable prismatic routes nearby |
| Scavengers nest in statues | Specific recovered knowledge domains |

---

### 11.6 Prismatic Routing

Once enough color exists, the sky participates:

- Certain routes only appear under specific sky angles
- Double-sunset alignment temporarily opens parts of the city
- Prism flares reveal hidden inscriptions or route markers
- Late game: sky becomes a dynamic timing system for hub interactions

---

## 12. Late-Game: Chromatic Pressure

Past a certain point, increased coloration stops feeling restorative and starts feeling **overfull**.

### Chromatic Pressure Meter

More recovered truth = increased pressure on hub's exhausted reality.

### 12.1 Chromatic Ruptures

Localized bursts where environment violently reasserts lost form:
- Statues crack, shed enormous slabs
- Floors flare into old mosaic geometry, then shatter
- Archways briefly become whole, then explode into dust
- Windows prism so hard they lance the environment with light

**Gameplay:** Changing traversal hazards, temporary blocked/unblocked paths, danger zones in formerly safe spaces.

---

### 12.2 Skyline Fatigue Events

The universe looks tired, old, overextended:
- Distant horizon tears
- Stars misalign
- Light blooms too long after sunset
- Whole towers silhouette against cosmic rupture flashes
- Debris lifts/trembles during pressure spikes

---

### 12.3 Overexposure

Too much fidelity causes certain truths to become painful or unstable:
- Some hub interactions only usable during lower-pressure periods
- Archive reads require bracing systems or partial dampening
- Too much active color in one district triggers failures elsewhere
- Player must decide which parts of the city to let burn brightest

---

### 12.4 Self-Destruction of Recovered Grandeur

- Colosal golden avenue lights fully, then chunks blow out of the paving
- Hero colossus regains pigment, then collapses from internal stress
- Observatory dome regains function, but sky beyond is visibly wrong

---

## 13. Phases Summary

| Phase | Name | State |
|-------|------|-------|
| 0 | **Ashen** | Nearly monochrome, broad silhouettes, simple traversal, vague intelligence, hub feels dead |
| 1 | **Differentiation** | Material classes separate, district identity readable, first reactivated mechanisms |
| 2 | **Recovery** | Colors return in domains, memory overlays begin, new routes/sky respond |
| 3 | **Saturation** | Magnificent city, rich symbolism, wildlife informative, campaign intelligence rich |
| 4 | **Overfull** | Chromatic pressure spikes, ruptures, fractures, collapsing grandeur, most beautiful and most endangered |

**Emotional curve:** dead → readable → wondrous → excessive → unstable
