# Feral Defense Remnants

---

## Core Concept

Feral Defense Remnants are old authority without living judgment. They are broken security systems, compliance frames, damaged exosuits, abandoned garrison hardware, and defense protocols still enforcing rules no one remembers.

They are not a society. They are institutional violence after the institution died. They should feel official, damaged, relentless, and eerie.

---

## Core Belief

None. That is the horror. They do not believe. They execute.

---

## Relationship to the Severing / Unnarrival

Feral Defense Remnants do not understand the Severing. They are what remains when authority fails to arrive but protocol continues. Orders did not update. Command did not arrive. Evacuation did not complete. Recognition tables did not sync. The final override never came. So they continue.

They are the machinery of arrival stuck in a dead loop.

---

## What They Are Right About

Some of what they guard is still dangerous. Their protocols may be obsolete, but not always baseless. The old world sealed many things for a reason.

---

## What They Are Wrong About

They cannot reinterpret context. They enforce protocol after context has died. They may defend empty rooms, kill authorized personnel, enforce evacuation routes into collapsed tunnels, or attack survivors because old identity tables no longer match.

---

## Relationship to Ash-Bell

Some remnants still enforce Ash-Bell directives. They may guard sealed doors, route closures, relay burns, or denial corridors without understanding why.

---

## Relationship to the Unnarrival

They do not use the term intentionally. Late-game systems may contain corrupted command language:
- `ARRIVAL WINDOW CLOSED`
- `UNNARRIVAL SAFEGUARD ACTIVE`
- `DO NOT COMPLETE ROUTE`
- `RECIPIENT LOCK ACTIVE`
- `ASH-BELL CORDON MAINTAINED`

These are not beliefs. They are leftovers.

---

## Relationship to the Hidden Entity

They may guard against it without knowing it. Some protocols may be ancient containment reflexes established by the hidden authority. A turret may be defending an empty room because the room is not truly empty in the way old systems measure arrival.

---

## Transitive Relationship to the Custodian

The Custodian may be recognized by one subsystem and rejected by another. This is not hatred. It is authority fragmentation.

---

## Visual Identity

Feral Defense Remnants should look like degraded formal systems. Security armor, riot frames, compliance drones, cracked shields, warning lights, exposed servos, faded authority markings, damaged exoskeletons, repaired plating, old suppressive weapons, breach tools, patrol optics. They should not look sleek — they are official hardware degraded through time and field repair.

**Color language:** Gunmetal, dark security green, faded white, black, dark gray, hazard orange, red warning lights, amber lamps, faint blue optics.

## Environmental Footprint

Feral Defense Remnants make spaces feel trapped in old emergency logic. They leave active warning lights, patrol lanes, kill-zone markings, sealed checkpoints, sparking doors, old security gates, half-working turrets, access scanners, barricades placed by protocol, evacuation arrows pointing into danger, bodies ignored unless obstructing pathing.

---

## Inspect Lines

- "THE WARNING LIGHT STILL WORKS."
- "AUTHORITY TABLE CORRUPTED."
- "THE PATROL ROUTE ENDS AT RUBBLE."
- "IT IS GUARDING AN EMPTY ROOM."
- "THE SCANNER RECOGNIZED YOU. THE GUN DID NOT."
- "THE FINAL OVERRIDE DID NOT ARRIVE."

---

## Enemy Behavior

**Pre-combat:** Patrol fixed routes, scan empty checkpoints, face sealed doors, repeat old formations, repair barriers, ignore enemies unless triggered, attack anything crossing a line.

**In combat:** Enforce zones, use predictable but dangerous patterns, activate old defenses, guard sealed objects, fail to adapt quickly, become more dangerous in prepared spaces.

---

## Gameplay Identity

Feral Defense Remnants attack movement and space. They create patrol puzzles, turret rooms, locked security lanes, predictable but punishing combat, old access conflicts, traps that imply former procedure, rooms where the safest action is observation before engagement. **Their damage is procedural and spatial.**
