---

## Core Concept

The Buried Kins are not monsters. They are descendants of sealed shelters, underground service crews, continuity bunkers, and survival populations that endured after the Severing by maintaining enclosed systems for generations.

They are human-first: practical, defensive, suspicious, and shaped by hard continuity. They are not cave ghouls, mutants, or boogeymen. Their tragedy is not that they became monstrous. Their tragedy is that they survived by becoming culturally sealed.

They are a people who kept the lights on after the world stopped coming back.

---

## Core Belief

> "We remained. That is proof enough."

They believe survival is legitimacy. Their tools, rituals, family structures, and leadership all descend from maintenance duty, shelter discipline, ration logic, and emergency continuity.

---

## Relationship to the Severing / Unnarrival

The Buried Kins understand the Severing as abandonment. Not cosmic theory — lived fact. The convoy did not arrive. The relief team did not arrive. The promised reopening did not arrive. So they remained.

Their culture formed around the moral authority of endurance.

---

## What They Are Right About

They did preserve life. They maintained systems others abandoned. They understand infrastructure as lived reality, not abstract doctrine. They are often better stewards of local machinery than outsiders.

---

## What They Are Wrong About

They may mistake isolation for truth. Because they survived inside a sealed reality, they may reject any history that threatens the meaning of that survival. The Custodian may reveal that their sacred wardens were maintenance supervisors, their founding law was a ration protocol, or their "ancestral promise" was an automated shelter message. That does not make them stupid. It makes the truth painful.

---

## Relationship to Ash-Bell

They may be descendants of those harmed by Ash-Bell authority. For them, Ash-Bell is not theology. It is inherited family reality. A command was issued. A door closed. The world did not come back. Their culture formed around enduring the consequences.

---

## Relationship to the Unnarrival

They may not use the word early. To them, the Unnarrival is not abstract — it is the shape of their lives. Later, if exposed, the term may be painful because it formalizes what they already knew: help did not come because arrival itself failed, was denied, or was prevented.

---

## Relationship to the Hidden Entity

They likely do not know the entity. But they may be its living evidence. Their communities may have been intentionally kept near Ash-Bell sites as witness populations, continuity ballast, containment anchors, living buffers — unwilling participants in Null Warrant strategy. This is morally awful and should emerge slowly.

---

## Transitive Relationship to the Custodian

The Custodian arrives late. That is the conflict. Even if he is right, even if he is trying to help, he represents the authority that did not arrive when needed.

---

## Visual Identity

The Buried Kins should look like an isolated human continuity culture. Patched shelter clothing, reinforced workwear, respirators, old utility jackets, lamp rigs, enclosure suits, gloves, belts, storage pouches, repaired civic gear, inherited bunker hardware. They should feel practical, communal, and defensive. **No horror poses, no monster silhouettes, no generic mutants.**

**Color language:** Bunker green, faded orange, work gray, earth brown, muted navy, rust, dirty cream, utility blue, dim red indicators, faded safety yellow.

## Environmental Footprint

Buried Kins make spaces livable. They leave sleeping partitions, ration shelves, repaired lights, carefully patched pipes, hand-painted wayfinding, water collection systems, family marks, tool racks, children's marks or domestic traces, maintained generators, blocked passages for safety, reused civic signage. They do not decorate like cultists. They adapt.

---

## Inspect Lines

- "THE PIPE WAS REPAIRED MANY TIMES."
- "THIS WAS A CHECKPOINT. NOW IT IS A KITCHEN."
- "SOMEONE LOWERED THE SIGN FOR A CHILD."
- "THE GENERATOR IS CLEAN."
- "THE HELP NOTICE WAS PAINTED OVER."
- "THEY STOPPED WAITING BEFORE THEY STOPPED LIVING."

---

## Enemy Behavior

**Pre-combat:** Repair lights, carry supplies, guard water sources, tend generators, escort technicians, retreat civilians offscreen, block passages, salvage carefully rather than strip randomly.

**In combat:** Defend territory, protect support units, fall back through prepared routes, guard machines they depend on, avoid unnecessary destruction, use traps, chokepoints, and maintenance access.

---

## Gameplay Identity

The Buried Kins challenge the player's assumption that every hostile group is evil. They create situations where enemies defend survival infrastructure, killing them may give access but damage moral context, nonlethal or avoidance options would matter later, recovered truth may destabilize them. **Their damage is relational.**
