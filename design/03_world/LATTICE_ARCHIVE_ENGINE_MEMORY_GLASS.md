# Lattice, Archive Engine, and Memory Glass Doctrine

**Project:** CUSTODIAN
**Status:** draft-for-canon-lock
**Authority:** Addendum to `design/03_world/LATTICE_DOCTRINE.md` and `design/03_world/GAME_PROTOCOLS_AND_WORLD_LORE.md`
**Purpose:** Define the relationship between the Lattice, Archive Engines, Memory Glass, provenance failure, and Pale intrusion.

---

## 1. Purpose

This document hardens the relationship between three core CUSTODIAN concepts:

* **The Lattice** — the protected continuity field / survivable reality pocket.
* **Archive Engines** — the infrastructure that inflates, reinforces, reconciles, and maintains the Lattice.
* **Memory Glass** — the mnemonic command-state substrate inside Archive Engines that preserves local provenance.

This document exists to prevent term drift.

The Lattice is not the engine.
The Archive Engine is not the Lattice.
Memory Glass is not generic data storage.

Together, they explain how a reality pocket holds together, how it fails, and why fragments of broken Memory Glass can contain incompatible but locally valid histories.

---

## 2. Canonical Relationship

### 2.1 The Lattice

The **Lattice** is the temporal/existential container that makes a bounded reality pocket survivable.

A Lattice is not a planet, colony, shield dome, simulation, or permanent world-state. It is a temporary continuity field imposed around a slice of place, history, witness, and meaning.

A Lattice:

* holds a bounded pocket of reality coherent enough to inhabit;
* preserves causal and historical continuity within that pocket;
* resists Pale intrusion for a finite time;
* depends on Archive infrastructure for reinforcement;
* eventually collapses.

A campaign world is not "a world saved by the player." It is a Lattice pocket kept alive long enough for something inside it to still matter.

### 2.2 Archive Engines

An **Archive Engine** is the machine-authority system that inflates, reinforces, and maintains a Lattice.

Archive Engines are not simple computers. They are continuity infrastructure: archive, adjudicator, stabilizer, provenance ledger, reconstruction engine, and field authority in one system.

An Archive Engine:

* reinforces a Lattice around a bounded reality pocket;
* maintains local provenance state;
* reconciles contradictions between object, origin, witness, time, use, and meaning;
* accepts or rejects recovered evidence;
* expands Lattice integrity through restored infrastructure and knowledge recovery;
* fails when contradictions exceed its ability to reconcile them.

Archive Engines do not permanently save worlds. They buy coherence.

### 2.3 Memory Glass

**Memory Glass** is the Archive Engine's mnemonic command-state substrate.

It is not ordinary glass and not ordinary memory. It is a physical provenance-bearing medium used by Archive Engines to preserve local reality state: records, authority traces, witness chains, command-state residues, contradiction maps, and operational decisions.

Memory Glass can store:

* local provenance state;
* command-state traces;
* witness relationships;
* validated origin chains;
* disputed or quarantined contradictions;
* Lattice anchor state;
* operational authority residues;
* reconstruction attempts.

Memory Glass is therefore part archive matter, part processor substrate, part field stabilizer, and part forensic medium.

It does not merely remember what happened. It helps determine which version of what happened the Lattice is still able to hold.

## 3. Provenance State

### 3.1 Definition

**Provenance** is the recoverable relationship between:

```text
object → origin → witness → time → use → meaning → authority
```

CUSTODIAN's world is not broken because records were lost. It is broken because the relationships that allow records to agree with reality were wounded.

The Great Severance damaged the substrate that lets events be commonly witnessed, remembered, sequenced, inherited, and trusted.

### 3.2 Local Provenance State

Each Archive Engine maintains a **local provenance state** for the Lattice it supports.

This state is not a static database. It is an active reconciliation model. It continuously asks:

* What exists here?
* Where did it come from?
* Who or what witnessed it?
* When did it occur?
* What authority governs it?
* What contradictions are known?
* Which contradictions can be contained?
* Which contradictions threaten Lattice integrity?

A stable Lattice does not require perfect truth. It requires enough reconciled provenance for reality to remain operational.

## 4. Pale Intrusion and Lattice Failure

### 4.1 The Pale

The **Pale** is the field-facing pressure of erasure, contradiction, and unreconciled absence pressing against a Lattice.

The Pale is not merely fog, void, radiation, corruption, or darkness. Those are presentation layers.

The Pale is what the player sees when the Lattice can no longer keep a place, object, witness, or event fully admitted into coherent reality.

The Pale attacks continuity by weakening relationships:

```text
source detaches from object
witness detaches from event
event detaches from sequence
name detaches from owner
authority detaches from origin
memory detaches from history
```

### 4.2 The Unarrival

**The Unarrival** is the deeper impossible source behind the Great Severance: a presence, event, saint, weaponized contradiction, or non-event that cannot fully enter history but still leaves consequences inside it.

The Unarrival is not to be overexplained in normal player-facing text. It should be encountered through symptoms: records that contradict themselves, artifacts that precede manufacture, witnesses that precede events, and histories that demand continuation without ever having begun.

### 4.3 Archive Failure

A Lattice begins to fail when its Archive Engine can no longer reconcile local provenance.

This does not always happen because the Engine loses power or is physically destroyed. The more important failure mode is contradiction overload.

The Engine receives multiple histories that are each locally valid but mutually incompatible.

Example:

```text
The gate was opened.
The gate was sealed before construction.
The gate was destroyed in a war that did not happen.
The gate requests maintenance from an authority that never existed.
```

A functioning Archive Engine can quarantine contradiction, defer interpretation, mark records contested, or restrict restoration.

A failing Archive Engine cannot.

When Pale intrusion reaches the Memory Glass, the substrate itself begins preserving contradictions as if each one were authoritative.

At that point, the Engine is no longer merely storing corrupted memory. It is reinforcing incompatible histories into the Lattice.

That is a direct mechanism of Lattice collapse.

## 5. Memory Glass Fracture

### 5.1 Fracture Event

When an Archive Engine fails to reconcile Pale intrusion, its Memory Glass can fracture along contradiction lines.

This is not simple shattering.

A Memory Glass fracture is a provenance failure made physical.

Each shard separates around a different incompatible reconstruction of the same place, object, event, or authority chain.

The result is not one broken record, but several records that cannot coexist.

### 5.2 What Each Shard Contains

A shard of Memory Glass may preserve:

* one valid-but-incomplete history;
* one failed reconciliation attempt;
* one quarantined contradiction;
* one command-state trace;
* one dead authority signal;
* one local version of a ruin;
* one witness chain that no longer agrees with the world;
* one impossible event that the Lattice could not reject.

The shard is valuable because it still contains provenance. It is dangerous because that provenance may be incompatible with the current field.

### 5.3 Why Shards Matter Mechanically

Memory Glass fragments should support gameplay because they are not generic crafting crystals.

They can justify:

* Archive upgrades;
* Lattice stabilization;
* contradiction analysis;
* Hub interpretation progress;
* sealed lore reconstruction;
* anomaly classification;
* dangerous knowledge unlocks;
* faction interest from groups obsessed with origin, witness, and authority.

Memory Glass is valuable because it contains the remains of decisions the Lattice once depended on.

## 6. Correct Item Framing

### 6.1 Canonical Resource Name

Use:

```text
Memory Glass Fragment
```

Use lowercase snake case in data where needed:

```text
memory_glass_fragment
```

Avoid alternate item names unless they are faction slang or degraded local terms.

### 6.2 Item Description — Preferred

**Memory Glass Fragment**
Brittle glass from an Archive Engine's mnemonic substrate. It once held local provenance in support of a Lattice. When Pale intrusion reached the engine, the glass fractured along histories the Lattice could no longer reconcile.

### 6.3 Short Variant

**Memory Glass Fragment**
A splinter of mnemonic glass from a failed Lattice's Archive Engine. Each fracture holds a different provenance for the same ruin.

### 6.4 More Ominous Variant

**Memory Glass Fragment**
Brittle Archive glass that once helped hold a Lattice coherent. Its fractures preserve several incompatible histories of the same place, each still claiming authority.

## 7. Player-Facing Presentation Rules

### 7.1 Do Not Explain Everything Directly

Player-facing text should rarely say:

```text
The Archive Engine stores local provenance in Memory Glass and failed to reconcile Pale intrusion.
```

That is doctrine language.

Player-facing artifacts should imply it through:

* broken terminal diagnostics;
* item descriptions;
* environmental contradictions;
* repeated but incompatible room layouts;
* map corruption;
* dead systems asking for impossible authorization;
* NPCs remembering mutually exclusive local histories;
* Memory Glass shards that describe the same place differently.

### 7.2 Approved Diagnostic Language

Use language like:

```text
PROVENANCE FAILURE:
OBJECT PRECEDES MANUFACTURE.
WITNESS PRECEDES EVENT.
EVENT PRECEDES WORLD.

LATTICE WARNING:
LOCAL HISTORY RESOLVES TO MULTIPLE AUTHORITIES.

ARCHIVE ENGINE:
COMMAND-STATE SUBSTRATE FRACTURED.
RECONCILIATION FAILED.
FIELD COHERENCE DECLINING.

MEMORY GLASS:
FRAGMENT RETAINS CONTRADICTORY LOCAL PROVENANCE.
HANDLE AS CONTESTED AUTHORITY.
```

### 7.3 Environmental Presentation

A failed Archive Engine site should show:

* fractured glass-like substrate embedded in machinery;
* partial reflections of rooms that do not match the current ruin;
* terminals displaying several incompatible event chains;
* Pale seepage around archive pylons or engine chambers;
* physical shards that seem to repel or desynchronize from one another;
* multiple local histories expressed as environmental overlays.

Memory Glass should feel like broken authority, not magic loot.

## 8. Anti-Patterns

Do not describe the Lattice as:

* a permanent shield;
* a simulation;
* a planet;
* a spaceship;
* a generic force field;
* a simple "continuity lattice" if the actual meaning is the protected reality pocket.

Do not describe Archive Engines as:

* ordinary servers;
* central processors;
* AI gods;
* databases;
* power generators only.

Do not describe Memory Glass as:

* generic crystal;
* hard drive glass;
* mana shard;
* simple archive storage;
* memory in the ordinary computer sense.

Do not imply:

* the Custodian can permanently save a world;
* Archive completion wins the campaign forever;
* Pale intrusion is merely environmental damage;
* contradictions are just bad records;
* Memory Glass breaks only after the Lattice has already failed.

The sharper canon is:

```text
Pale intrusion corrupts the Archive Engine's mnemonic substrate.
The Engine fails to reconcile local provenance.
Memory Glass fractures along incompatible histories.
That fracture can be part of how the Lattice fails.
```

## 9. System Design Implications

### 9.1 Lattice Integrity

Lattice integrity should be affected by actions that restore or damage provenance:

* restoring infrastructure;
* recovering verified records;
* stabilizing ARRN or equivalent relay anchors;
* resolving contradiction sites;
* extracting Memory Glass fragments;
* repairing Archive Engine components;
* preventing factions from overwriting or destroying contested evidence.

### 9.2 Memory Glass as Upgrade Material

Memory Glass should be used for upgrades that relate to:

* interpretation;
* archive reconstruction;
* Lattice stabilization;
* field radius;
* contradiction resistance;
* anomaly classification;
* Hub inference confidence.

Avoid using Memory Glass for ordinary weapon crafting unless the weapon effect is explicitly provenance-related.

### 9.3 Failed Lattice Sites

Collapsed Lattice sites should be major sources of Memory Glass.

This makes Memory Glass emotionally legible:

The player is harvesting failed attempts to keep future attempts alive.

---

## 10. Summary Doctrine

The Lattice is the survivable continuity field.
The Archive Engine is the machine-authority system that reinforces it.
Memory Glass is the Engine's mnemonic command-state substrate.
Provenance is the relationship between object, origin, witness, time, use, meaning, and authority.
The Pale breaks those relationships.
The Unarrival is the impossible root wound behind the Severance.
When an Archive Engine can no longer reconcile Pale intrusion, its Memory Glass fractures into incompatible histories.
Those fractures are not just remains of a failed Lattice. They may be one of the ways the Lattice failed.

Every Memory Glass shard is therefore both resource and wound: a usable fragment of authority from a reality pocket that could not continue.
